## Narrator decision (load-bearing — CIC ratify)

**Wanjiru Kamau** narrates Vol-3 in first-person past tense, continuing the staff-history convention Anna founded. Wanjiru picks up the convention from inside the standards-body work — she is writing because Anna asked her to, and because the institutional record requires what the regulatory filings cannot say.

**Rationale (per series-arc § 5.2):** Wanjiru's institutional thinking is the substrate that lets Sunfish survive centralization attempts across centuries. She is the Egwene-equivalent in the WoT mapping. The drafter writes her as already-thinking-past-the-boat in Vol-2; Vol-3 IS that thinking executed.

**Voice differential from Anna (Vol-2):**
- More restrained interiority. Wanjiru does not narrate feelings; she narrates the framework that surrounds them. The reader infers the interior from the institutional pattern.
- Kikuyu-rhythm humming threads as Anna registered it in Vol-2 — Wanjiru's narrator does not call it out; the reader recognizes the same cadence.
- Cucu's-notebook (Vol-2 ch15 plant) becomes the chapter-spanning structural object — Wanjiru writes alongside the proverbs her grandmother left her; one proverb per chapter heading.
- The "procedure is what the procedure holds against" register (Vol-2 ch06+ch15) is canonical.

## Back cover (DRAFT — Wanjiru's voice)

Three years after the *Nansen* surfaced at Punta Arenas, I was the one who said yes when the consortium asked who would chair the standards body.

I'm **Wanjiru Kamau**, and I had been a relay-operations officer for fifteen years before that. The framework that grew out of MERIDIAN-7 — the vendor-attestation requirements, the firmware-transparency rules, the supply-chain-audit doctrine — those did not draft themselves. The institutional record will tell you they were authored by a working group of seventeen architects, signatories from twelve consortium members, and a chair who came up from the boats. The institutional record is correct.

What the institutional record does not say is that the working group met for the first time in a hotel function room in Helsinki on the twelfth of January, that I had been at the consortium ports for three days before that drafting language that the working group then took apart sentence by sentence over forty-one hours, and that Helvetia Trust's representative arrived on the second day with a counter-proposal whose deviations from our draft were precisely calibrated to make the standard unenforceable without making the deviation visible.

The deviations were visible to one person at the table.

I'm writing this because the framework will outlast me and the people who made it possible deserve to be named while I can still name them. Anna handed me the convention four years ago in a wardroom on a research submarine that no longer exists; I am writing in it now because that is what comes after returning, and because the next time the Forsaken try this — and they will — the record needs to say what they tried and what it cost to refuse.

The framework holds.

The Forsaken are not going to stop.

## Register commitments (load-bearing)

1. **First-person POV throughout.** Wanjiru narrates. Past tense (institutional staff history written 18 months after the founding events).
2. **Institutional rhythm.** Sentences are framework-aware. Where Anna's voice digressed through personal-history, Wanjiru's voice digresses through *what the procedure was* and *what it became*.
3. **Restrained interiority.** Wanjiru does not narrate feelings; she narrates structural responses to the situations that produce feelings. The reader infers the rest. (Stronger restraint than Anna's voice in Vol-2.)
4. **Technical content as institutional fact.** Standards language, regulatory citations, vendor-attestation specifications — explained the way Wanjiru explains them: as the structural reality the room must operate against.
5. **Proverbs at chapter heads.** Each chapter opens with one of Wanjiru's grandmother's proverbs (Kikuyu original + Wanjiru's translation). The proverb names what the chapter will demonstrate without describing it. (Vol-2 ch15 plant: *the steward does not chase her own heart.*)
6. **Joel + Anna recur, do not return.** Both appear in correspondence, in the regulatory record, in Wanjiru's recall. Neither is a present-day scene partner. The reader feels the absence as the cost of the founding.
7. **Stefan as institutional shadow, not present-tense antagonist.** Stefan is what the framework is being designed against. He appears in the room three times across the book; he speaks twice. Wanjiru registers him the way Anna registered him in Vol-2 ch18 — at the institutional register, without narrating what was not said.

## Plot anchors (DRAFT — CIC ratify)

- **Inciting incident:** consortium response to Anna's regulatory filing arrives; Working Group recommendation: form a standards body with chair-from-the-boats requirement. Wanjiru is asked.
- **Central institutional contest:** Helvetia Trust's counter-proposal in Helsinki — vendor-attestation language calibrated to be unenforceable. Wanjiru's framework refuses; Helvetia withdraws; framework adopts; Helvetia returns at the regulatory layer.
- **First architectural test:** the second Sunfish-architecture mission (operator: a research consortium other than the consortium that funded MERIDIAN-7) experiences a supply-chain compromise that the new framework's forensic-attestation requirement catches *before* the mission ships. The framework's first prevented incident.
- **Internal crisis:** Wanjiru's daughter (introduced — new character, age 14 at book start, 17 at end) enters Wanjiru's own institutional record by asking the question Wanjiru has not let herself ask: whether the framework requires being away from one's life for the years required to found it. Anna's Diana-thread is mirrored by Wanjiru's Imani-thread.
- **The Hiroshi payoff (Vol-2 forward-plant `hiroshi-unfinished-line`):** Hiroshi appears mid-book at a standards-body conference. He has finished the drawing he started on the *Nansen*. He gives it to Wanjiru. The drawing is referenced; not described in detail. The reader who remembers the Vol-2 ch18 notebook moment recognizes what closed.
- **External antagonist (Stefan + capital):** Helvetia Trust + successors attempt institutional capture at three layers — vendor attestation language; standards-body voting structure; jurisdictional arbitrage on enforceability. Each attempt is refused; each refusal shifts the next attack vector.
- **Anna's continuing absence:** Anna's staff history (the artifact the Vol-2 reader read) is now in circulation. Wanjiru references it as "the book"; the consortium uses it as evidentiary background; Stefan's faction attempts to discredit Anna's record. The framework Wanjiru is building is partly a response to that attack.
- **Climax shape:** the framework's first vote — vendor-attestation requirement adopted with a 13-2 vote (Helvetia + one captured member dissenting). The institutional founding moment.
- **Resolution shape:** Wanjiru hands the chair to her successor on a planned schedule; institution outlasts founder; Joel and Anna are at the handover ceremony; Imani has finished her first year of secondary school. Book closes on the proverb Wanjiru began with, now paid.

## Anti-patterns (what this book is not)

- Not Vol-2 reheated. The under-ice tension does not return; the new tension is *in the room*.
- Not procedural-thriller. Standards work is genuinely slow; the book leans into the pace and finds gravity in it.
- Not legal-thriller register. Wanjiru is not a lawyer; she is an operator-turned-institution-builder. The voice does not translate to courtroom-drama cadence.
- Not nostalgic for Vol-2. Anna and Joel recur in correspondence and reference; the book does not reach backward.
- Not redemption-arc for Astrid (premature; that's Vol-4).
- Not series-arc-explicit. Per series-arc § 8 forbidden registers, Vol-3 prose does NOT name the centuries-long trajectory. Wanjiru does not foretell. The standards body is being founded; what it becomes lives in the reader's retroactive sense, not in Wanjiru's narration.
