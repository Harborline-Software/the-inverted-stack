## What this document is

A Vol-3 **authoring plan**, not the spine itself. The spine names the shape;
this plan names the chapter-by-chapter sequence + ICM-stage scheduling + parallel
workstreams + halt conditions. It is the bridge from "we know what Vol-3 is" (spine)
to "we can dispatch chapter drafts" (each chapter has an outline ready for
chapter-drafter at ICM 02–03).

This plan does NOT supersede the SPINE. If a conflict surfaces between this plan and
the SPINE, the SPINE wins; this plan gets revised.

## Structural inheritance from Vol-2

What Vol-3 inherits unchanged:

- **First-person past-tense narration** — Wanjiru is the narrator; she writes 18
  months after the founding events (same retrospective-frame as Anna's Vol-2
  staff-history convention).
- **Audiobook-native production** — 30–45 minute audiobook chapters, ~16–20
  chapters target, voice-driven (Bobiverse / *Project Hail Mary* shelf with
  institutional-restraint substitute for Vol-2's under-ice tension).
- **No series-arc-explicit language** — Vol-3 prose does NOT name the
  centuries-long trajectory; series-arc § 8 forbidden registers apply.
- **Joel and Anna recur in correspondence, do not appear in present-tense scenes**
  — same discipline as Stefan in Vol-2 (institutional shadow, not scene partner).
- **ICM pipeline** — same `icm/outline` → `icm/draft` → ... → `icm/approved`
  workflow. Chapter-drafter, technical-reviewer, prose-reviewer, research-assistant
  agents available.

What Vol-3 introduces new:

- **Wanjiru's narrator voice** — restraint-tier above Anna's; institutional rhythm
  (sentences are framework-aware); Kikuyu proverbs at chapter heads (one per chapter,
  voiced in original + translation in audiobook).
- **Institutional-record vs diary register** — the chapter-spanning structural
  device is Wanjiru's *Cucu's notebook* (planted in Vol-2 Ch 15). Chapters open
  with a proverb, then the chapter body operates in institutional-record register,
  with diary interludes at specific moments.
- **Three Helvetia institutional contests** — vendor-attestation language;
  standards-body voting structure; jurisdictional arbitrage on enforceability.
  Each is a load-bearing chapter arc.
- **Imani thread** — Wanjiru's 14-17 year-old daughter; the institutional cost in
  family-time named without forecast (mirrors Anna's niece thread in Vol-2 but
  closer to the narrator's center of gravity).
- **The Hiroshi payoff** — Vol-2 plant `hiroshi-unfinished-line` pays off mid-book
  at a standards-body conference; the drawing is given to Wanjiru; not described
  in detail; the Vol-2 ch18 notebook-moment reader recognizes what closed.

## Voice canonicalization plan

### Voice differential from Anna (Vol-2)

The SPINE specifies Wanjiru as **more restrained interiority** + **institutional
rhythm** + **proverbs at chapter heads** + **Kikuyu-rhythm humming threads
implicit, not stated**.

Translating that into authoring checklist for chapter-drafter:

1. **No "let me tell you about" turns.** Anna's voice opens beats with "Let me
   tell you about [character]"; Wanjiru does not. Wanjiru opens beats with
   the proverb or with the institutional-context fact.
2. **No pastry / bakery / culinary digressions.** Anna's voice has the
   bakery-as-emotional-anchor register; Wanjiru does NOT have that signature.
   Wanjiru's signature is what she observes about institutional rooms
   (the chair's posture; what was on the table at the close of the session;
   the specific wording of the closing minute) — observation at the
   institutional layer, not the personal layer.
3. **No "the call I am still not sure about" hedges.** Anna's voice carries
   that retrospective hedge as a tonal device; Wanjiru does not hedge in that
   register. When Wanjiru does not know, she names what she does not know.
   When she chose, she names what she chose. When she was wrong, she names
   what she was wrong about. (Closer to Joel's discipline than Anna's
   register, while not adopting Joel's voice.)
4. **Parentheticals are rare.** Anna's voice carries parentheticals as a
   wry-aside device; Wanjiru's voice does not. When Wanjiru includes
   something, it is in the main clause. (The proverb-chapter-heads carry
   the "aside" function structurally; the prose itself does not.)
5. **Em-dashes are used surgically.** Where Anna might use 3–4 em-dashes
   per chapter as voice-tells, Wanjiru uses 1–2 per chapter. The discipline
   of restraint extends to punctuation.
6. **Sentence length skews longer.** Anna mixes short punchy lines with
   long lyrical lines; Wanjiru's default sentence is mid-length and
   declarative. Short punchy lines are reserved for the moments that
   demand them.
7. **Proverbs at chapter heads carry voice-canonical Kikuyu.** The drafter
   must research authentic Kikuyu proverbs (research-assistant agent) and
   render the chapter-head in Kikuyu original + Wanjiru's translation +
   (optional) brief notational context. The translation is Wanjiru's voice
   — close, idiomatic, not literal.

### Voice-pilot recommendation

Vol-2 has voice pilots at `vol-2/_voice-drafts/janeway-pilot/` +
`towles-pilot/`. Vol-3 should commission a parallel **Wanjiru-pilot**
authoring ch 1 (the letter) at full draft + voice quality before any other
chapter ships. The pilot's purpose: ratify the voice differential against
Vol-2's Anna in a single 5.5k-word sample before locking the voice for the
remaining 15 chapters.

If the pilot lands clean (PAO + CIC listen-test approves), the rest of
Vol-3 proceeds to chapter-drafter dispatch. If the pilot misses (Anna's
voice keeps leaking; Wanjiru reads as Anna-with-East-African-affect rather
than as a distinct voice), the pilot iterates until the voice differential
holds.

**Halt condition for Vol-3:** if 3 pilot iterations do not produce a
voice-distinct Wanjiru, surface to CIC for narrator-decision revisit
(possible fallback: split-POV Anna+Wanjiru; OR new narrator entirely; OR
the voice rotation isn't the right structural choice for the series).
