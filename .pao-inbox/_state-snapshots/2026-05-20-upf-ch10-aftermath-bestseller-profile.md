## Findings Summary

### Top 3 Strengths

**S1 — The Havránek chain sentence.** The long accumulation at section 2's opening ("What it does not tell you is that the mission was cut short because a schema-migration limitation...") is the chapter's defining prose moment. It performs the suppression it names: the weight of not saying it compressed into a single syntactic container. This is the kind of sentence that gets quoted in reviews.

**S2 — Stefan vs. Astrid differentiation.** The chapter resists the easy move of making Stefan and Astrid identical moral failures. Stefan is a man under pressure he could not name; Astrid is an institution naming what it will not say. "The two non-disclosures were of different kinds... The shape was identical." The distinction earns the structural parallel and prevents the antagonists from collapsing into a single archetype.

**S3 — Aytib Qoy.** The grandmother's naming practice as Anna's governing epistemic inheritance is structurally generous — it grounds the chapter's entire analytical architecture in a concrete childhood memory without sentimentalizing either the memory or the analysis. The Uzbek words without gloss are a precise register choice.

### Top 3 Weaknesses (with actionable fixes)

**W1 — Anaphora + tricolon cluster in the three-inverses section.**
Cited: "Three architectural inverses. One in disclosure. One in disciplined limit. One in institutional refusal." followed within six paragraphs by "three different operational shapes; three different professional vocabularies; three different framings."
Fix: Break the fragment-triplet. Replace with continuous prose: "I had selected for the same property in three different vocabularies: Joel's in the protocol, Priya's in the lab, Wanjiru's at the standards layer." Drop the tricolon close or vary the third element's structure so the three-beat rhythm is not doubled.

**W2 — Opening wardroom-hum drone over-extended.**
Cited: "The wardroom hum was the wardroom hum" appears as a close at paragraph 1, then recurred at the Maria exit, and the opening paragraphs' paratactic catalogue runs four paragraphs.
Fix: Trim the opening to two paragraphs, reserving the hum-repetition for the Maria exit and the final close. Let Anna's physical specificity (the dented cup handle, Day 22 stopping-noticing) carry the disorientation without the refrain appearing before it has earned recurrence value.

**W3 — Self-referential frame used twice.**
Cited: "I am, tonight, writing it down for the first time" (section 2 close) and "I have been writing the file on the desk under three hundred meters of ice in the mode my father's mother gave me" (section 5).
Fix: Per ANNA-VOICE.md AP-6, pick one. The section 5 instance is structurally the more load-bearing one (it ties grandmother to the chapter's analytical project). Cut "I am, tonight, writing it down for the first time" from section 2 close; let the fact of the writing be implicit from the file-open sequence already established. The section 2 close can end instead on "The shape is what I went home with from Bremerhaven. The shape is what I have carried for five years." — full stop, no frame-activation needed.

### Literary Devices Grade: **B**

Strong: symbolic architecture (non-disclosure as shape), foreshadowing (Stefan in conspiratorial register sets up his later active antagonist role), irony (Anna selected for the dispositional property before she had vocabulary for it), structural device (private-file as confessional frame). Weaknesses: AP-2 and AP-4 violations; AP-6 double-frame; opening drone slightly over-extended. A well-executed chapter with two correctable craft errors.
