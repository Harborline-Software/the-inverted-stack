## Findings: Top 3 Weaknesses

**Weakness 1 — The "at the standing" formula is a noise floor. Actionable.**
The phrase "at the standing" or "at the standing [noun]" appears approximately 35-40 times across 4,963 words. In scene 1 alone: "at the standing medical level," "at the polar-operations console at the standing rotation," "at the standing post-dinner hour," "at the standing position," "at the standing capture," "the standing IAA institutional-stationery," "the standing surface-transit rate." The construction was calibrated to carry the mission's procedural register, but at this density it stops reading as a character's operational vocabulary and starts reading as a mechanical formula that the author is leaning on as a voice substitute. ANNA-VOICE anti-pattern #5 (motif phrases more than once per chapter) does not technically apply here because these are operational compound phrases rather than a single repeated expression — but the cumulative effect is the same: the reader stops hearing it.

**Fix:** Reduce "at the standing" by approximately 60% (from ~38 to ~15) across the chapter. Replace with specific nouns where the precision earns the specificity ("at the surface-transit rate"), drop the formula entirely where it adds no information ("at the standing post-dinner hour" → "the post-dinner hour"), and trust the reader to carry the operational register through the scenes without constant reinforcement. The formula is doing safety-blanket work rather than character work.

**Weakness 2 — The forensic analysis scene (Joel + Wanjiru five-item walk-through) lacks interiority between analytical steps. Actionable.**
The five-item structure is procedurally clean and narratively honest. The problem is that Anna's presence at the table is nearly invisible across the twelve paragraphs of forensic exchange. She appears at the scene's opening and at its close, but between those frame points, she is not marking what she is observing. The outline calls this the chapter's "structural anchor," which puts weight on a scene where Anna's voice is at its quietest. The forensic dialogue between Joel and Wanjiru is correct — their voices are differentiated, the analysis is rigorous without being resolved — but the reader needs one or two Anna-interior beats inside the scene to register that she is at the table reading both of them while they work, not merely transcribing.

**Fix:** Insert one Anna-interior observation after Joel's first reading-pair (after "The reading is mundane") and one after Wanjiru's low-frequency-component walk-through (after "The component is in the data"). Neither should interpret the analysis; both should register what Anna notices about how these two people are doing the work — Joel's body, Wanjiru's pen-stroke rate, the tempo of the room. 60-80 words each. This gives the scene an Anna-at-the-table register without upstaging the forensic dialogue.

**Weakness 3 — The OSS-funding-asymmetry reflective section is architecturally correct but over-stated. Actionable.**
The two paragraphs after the first scene divider (beginning "The paragraph was about the OSS-funding-asymmetry at the architectural level") are written at instructional register — the prose explains the point three times with slight variation. "Off-the-shelf had been the only choice. The choice was a property of small-OSS-project-and-commodity-vendor reality. The choice was not a failure." Then: "The architecture had done the architecture's part." Then: "The architecture had done what the architecture had been specified to do." The outline explicitly warns "without preaching it" — but these paragraphs preach at low volume, which is still preaching. The contrast clause ("The selective-corporate-retention model did not preserve...") adds information, but the three-beat restatement pattern immediately before it has already spent the insight.

**Fix:** Cut the middle paragraph of the three-paragraph block entirely (the one beginning "The architecture's response to the choice was the response..."). The first paragraph establishes the constraint; the third paragraph ("The architecture had done the architecture's part...") can carry both its own weight and the cut paragraph's weight in the resulting shorter form. This trims approximately 100 words from the section and removes the preaching register without losing the architectural claim.
