## Stage 0 — Discovery

Sources read this pass: chapter file (v1, 2026-05-16 Anna-voice rewrite), `SPINE.md`, `CHAPTER-OUTLINE.md` §Ch 15 entry, `canon.yaml`, `ANNA-VOICE.md` (full). Cerebrum and fleet-conventions consulted for Do-Not-Repeat.

**Discovery summary.** This chapter does something structurally rare in the volume: it runs in Wanjiru's first-person operational register for roughly 60% of its length, then hands back to Anna's staff-history register for the closing arc. That POV negotiation is the chapter's defining technical risk. The chapter also carries the volume's most explicit "three threat models / same shape" thesis articulation — which is both its greatest intellectual payoff and its greatest tonal danger.

## Stage 1 — Plan

### 1. Context and Why

Ch 15 is the second of the two "discipline reveals" in Act 3 (Ch 13 = Priya / Saidar under routine; Ch 14 = Saidin under physical stress; Ch 15 = Saidar under environmental and capability stress). It must land the forensic-substrate property as operationally real for the first time, seed the firmware-deviation question for Ch 17, and sustain Wanjiru's register as the voice her standards-body chair arc requires. The chapter comes immediately after the cascade climax of Ch 14, so reader energy is high but the register must shift from action to deliberate, institutional processing.

### 2. Success Criteria

**PASS conditions:**
- The forensic-substrate property feels earned, not asserted — reader registers it through what Wanjiru does, not through what she says about it.
- The three-fold structural-similarity recognition (political / environmental / capability) lands as an interior moment, not a lecture.
- The M-PESA flashback grounds Wanjiru's institutional instincts in specific scene rather than biography summary.
- The firmware-deviation question opens cleanly as a held mystery — specific enough to create tension, vague enough to not over-resolve.
- The Anna POV sections feel like her voice, not a tonal echo of Wanjiru's register.
- The Cucu proverb beat lands as genuine character without feel of narrative machinery.
- The Joel / Wanjiru exchange at 1714 earns its weight as the nuclear-navy register meeting institutional discipline.
- The final diary entry operates at a register the reader understands the official record cannot carry.

**FAILED conditions:**
- If the three-threat-model passage reads as a thesis paragraph rather than an interior recognition, the chapter has broken the "software as gravity" discipline.
- If Wanjiru's voice is indistinguishable from Anna's, the chapter has failed its POV mandate.
- If the Nairobi flashback runs longer than the chapter's frame allows for a secondary character's backstory, it has colonized a chapter that belongs to the mission-present.

## Top 3 Weaknesses (with Actionable Fixes)

**Weakness 1 — The "discipline was the discipline" tautological closures.**
The chapter ends three major sections with a variant of "The X was the X" as a closing beat. End of the Joel-exchange section: "The institutional discipline had been the discipline at the comm node since 0322. The institutional discipline was the discipline now. The discipline was the discipline." This is three sentences where one carries the weight. The redundancy is not Wanjiru's register — it is the prose straining to land a moment through repetition rather than specificity.

**Fix:** Cut the triple-close at the Joel-exchange section to a single sentence. "The institutional discipline was the discipline." Or better: cut the sentence entirely and let the scene end on Joel's "Wanjiru" / "Joel" exchange, which already lands the point. Apply the same compression to any other triple-close instance. Target: no more than one "X was the X" close per chapter.

**Weakness 2 — The Nairobi flashback runs two paragraphs past its arrival.**
Lines 232-244 after the narrative climax of the "no" scene. The flashback earns its presence through the specific scene (the regulator, the bank executive, the four junior engineers, the volume at which she said no). It loses momentum in the paragraph beginning "The architecture had been built against political coercion" where the chapter begins to explain what the architecture was specified against — work that the chapter's other sections already do. The analytical paragraph doubles what the reader has already been shown.

**Fix:** Cut or compress the two explanatory paragraphs at the end of the Nairobi flashback (lines 232-244) to one transition sentence that returns to the mission-present without summarizing the three-fold recognition. The recognition itself is earned in the following section at the comm node; the flashback does not need to name it before the body moment delivers it. This is a clean 200-word cut.

**Weakness 3 — The "account would carry" enumeration in Anna's 2304 wardroom passage runs too long.**
Lines 402-403. Anna describes what the day's account will carry across eight clauses. The list format is doing Anna-voice work — it is the accumulation structure ANNA-VOICE uses for Anna registering events — but eight items in a single sentence tips from deliberate accumulation into inventory. More problematically, the list structure risks the "commentary on Anna's own narration habits" anti-pattern (ANNA-VOICE §Anti-patterns, item 7): Anna narrating the narration she is about to produce.

**Fix:** Trim the "account would carry" enumeration to three or four items — the most load-bearing ones. Specifically: the architecture as its own investigative tool, the three-fold recognition in Wanjiru's face, and the relay holding against the temptation. The others (the watch handoff, the post-cascade pass, the crew adaptation) are covered by the chapter's prior narration and do not need to be named in the summary. Cut approximately 100-150 words.

## Single Highest-Leverage Edit

**Compress the forensic-query timestamp arithmetic section (Phase 5, approximately lines 162-198).**

The forty-eight-hour rule violation is the chapter's forensic thesis. The reader needs to understand three things: (1) there is a rule; (2) the rule was violated by approximately eight hours; (3) Wanjiru cannot tell whether this is routine or not. The chapter currently takes approximately 700 words to deliver these three things — the audit log event, the timestamps in full, the arithmetic from both directions (fifty-six hours; thirty-seven hours; eight hours; nine hours after a recheck), and the three layers of institutional qualification.

A reader who trusts Wanjiru does not need to verify the arithmetic. A reader who needs to verify the arithmetic will do it once, not three times. The current passage walks through the calculation from at least two angles, then logs the conclusion at length.

**Reduce this section to 400 words.** Keep: the specific timestamps (they are canonically important for Ch 17 resolution); the forty-eight-hour rule as a named institutional rule; the eight-hour deviation as the question; and the closing log entry in Wanjiru's voice. Cut: the re-derivation of the arithmetic from the second angle; the extended qualification of "routine vs. not routine" (it appears in both the interior narration and the log entry — one instance is sufficient); the paragraph beginning "The rule had been violated by approximately eight hours. The violation was small" (the log entry covers this more effectively).

This single edit resolves the chapter's most significant audiobook-pacing risk without touching the chapter's structural or thematic architecture.
