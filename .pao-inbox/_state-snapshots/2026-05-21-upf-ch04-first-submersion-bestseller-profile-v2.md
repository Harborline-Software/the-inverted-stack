## Weaknesses (top 3, with fixes)

1. **Carry-cluster penultimate paragraph (BREACH — confirmed present).**
Live grep count: 3 `carry*` instances; all concentrated in the penultimate wardroom-to-bridge passage. The full sentence: "The crew below the bridge plates were carrying it now without yet knowing they were carrying it." Omniscient knowing-captain over unknowing crew. Prohibited by ANNA-VOICE.md: "We are all of us" universal claims; POV contamination into another character's head. The broader paragraph has 4 carry-variants preceding this sentence, amplifying the rhetorical weight.
**Fix (same as v1):** Cut the final sentence entirely. The three preceding sentences ("The architecture would carry it. The comm officer would carry it. The Mission Director would carry it.") already verge on Janeway-rhetoric but are first-person defensible as Anna naming what she will hold. The crew-sentence crosses the line by asserting their interior state. Cut it; close on "The Mission Director would carry it." or replace the entire carry-cluster with a physical image (e.g., "I went forward to the bridge." — the very next line — does the job).

2. **Scene 6 recapitulation overlong (near-breach — confirmed present).**
The architecture-manifest paragraph (lines 232-233 in source, line 103-104 post-strip) enumerates all seven morning exchange items across 11+ lines. ANNA-VOICE.md: she names them and moves on. The "I had needed to install all of those things in my own head" landing is correct; the enumeration before it is scaffolding.
**Fix (same as v1):** Compress to four items. Cut the enumeration after "the schema stable." The reader has retained the morning; Anna does not need to restate it. Trust the landing.

3. **"I had hired both of them for the same reason" doubled (marginal — new precision).**
Live grep confirms: "I had hired both of them" appears once explicitly (Wanjiru-recognition scene); "I had hired them for the same reason" echoes it in the wardroom passage. The first instance is the chapter's emotional peak and earns its place. The second (in the carry-cluster paragraph) is the echo-confirm of it — a smaller version of the same observation used as transition into the Janeway-rhetoric penultimate. Cutting the carry-cluster per Fix 1 above removes this doubling automatically. No additional edit needed.
