## Top 3 Weaknesses (v2, with live-grep citations and fixes)

**1. Register family hard-cap violation (5 instances; cap is ≤3).** Live count: register (noun/verb, 3×), registered (1×), registering (1×). Distributed across lines 66, 78 (×3 in same paragraph), and 82 (×2 in same paragraph). The Day-14 coda alone contains 3 of the 5 instances — "registered as the same property / They were always going to register that way" plus the foreshadowing instance at line 78 "register on the rack-power log." The violation is 2 above cap.

Fix: (a) Line 78 foreshadowing — "register on the rack-power log and the survey-line ledger" → "appear on the rack-power log and the survey-line ledger" or "land on the rack-power log." (b) Line 82 Day-14 coda — "the architecture's authority and the architect's discipline registered as the same property" → "...resolved as the same property" or "...turned out to be the same property." "They were always going to register that way" → "They were always going to land that way" or "They were always the same property." Two substitutions bring the total to 3, at the cap.

**2. "Noted" over-concentration (7 instances; 3 in a single paragraph).** Anti-pattern #5 (motif-phrase cap: no phrase more than once per chapter once it starts reading as a device) applies. The 7 instances across lines 46, 56, 60, 70, 78 (×3) are individually appropriate — Anna's careful noting is on-character — but 3 in the same post-call paragraph (line 78: "I noted it... I noted the silence... I noted that, too") makes the tic audible. A reader who has listened to the whole chapter hears the third "I noted" on line 78 and stops hearing Anna; they hear the narrator reaching for a word.

Fix: Line 78 — reduce the 3 instances to 1. "I noted it" (after "I accepted it from him") → "I took that in." "I noted the silence but left it alone" → "I left it alone." "I noted that, too" (after Joel having read the team) → "I marked it." This removes 2 of the 3 same-paragraph instances, bringing the chapter total to 5 — still above the ideal but no longer reading as a tic.

**3. Two anti-pattern #7 instances (narration commentary on own habits).** Live count: 2 confirmed AP-7 instances. Line 44: "I am going to write something about my mother here that I have not, until now, written down." Line 74: "I am going to say something here that I have not said in print before. Plainly, because plainly is the only way it works." Both are self-conscious flaggings of what Anna is about to do. ANNA-VOICE.md rule 7: "Anna becoming self-conscious about her narrator-voice in front of the reader. Cut. The reader notices on their own."

The line-44 instance is the less dispensable of the two — it introduces a passage the reader might otherwise experience as an unexpected digression into personal history, and the framing helps. The line-74 instance is more exposed: "Plainly, because plainly is the only way it works" is meta-commentary on Anna's communication style, which is exactly what AP-7 prohibits. Fix: Line 74 — cut the opening two sentences entirely. The paragraph works without them. Begin at "The man who failed me on my last mission did not say what Joel said." The passage announces itself by what it does; Anna does not need to announce it first.

## Single Highest-Leverage Edit (v2)

**Resolve the register family hard-cap violation.** Two substitutions in line 82 (Day-14 coda): "registered as the same property" → "resolved as the same property"; "register that way" → "land that way." This corrects the hard violation (5→3), addresses the most concentrated instance (3 in the coda), and preserves the foreshadowing use at line 78 (which is structurally load-bearing as a plant). This is the one edit that moves the chapter from a hard-cap violation to a clean pass. Everything else — "noted" reduction, AP-7 trimming — is improvement work; only the register substitution corrects an outright violation.
