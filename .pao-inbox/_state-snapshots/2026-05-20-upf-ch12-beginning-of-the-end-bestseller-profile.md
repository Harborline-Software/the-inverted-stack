## Stage 1 — The Five CORE Sections

### 1. Context & Why

Ch 12 closes Act II by dramatizing the transition from a surveilled, surface-connected mission into a fully isolated ten-day final leg. The chapter's job is to make the reader feel the stakes of that isolation — not through statement, but through accumulated behavioral signals from Diego, Joel, Priya, Wanjiru, and Anna herself. Sunfish architecture (schema discipline, firmware audit, sync dormancy) is the substrate; Mission as Survival is the rail.

**Problem:** The current v1 draft executes the procedural layer with technical precision but has not yet been rewritten in the locked Bobiverse/Anna-voice register. The voice is competent naval prose but lacks the wry first-person parentheticals, self-aware emotional compression, and conversational rhythm that define Anna's narrator.

## Top 3 Weaknesses (with Fixes)

**Weakness 1 — "The X was the X" refrain is the chapter's dominant sound.**
Instances: "the hum was the hum" (4), "the boat is the boat" (3), "the bridge was the bridge" (2), "the discipline was the discipline" (2), "the tea is the tea" (1 — this one earns it), "the chart was the chart" (1). Per ANNA-VOICE.md anti-pattern #5, maximum one instance per motif phrase per chapter. In this chapter, the refrain operates as a draft placeholder for specific sensory or behavioral detail.

**Fix:** In each instance except the tea-line, replace with one specific sensory or behavioral detail. "The hum was the hum" → "The engine note was the engine note the boat carried when the prop was at the low-speed ratio — the one that meant we were settled." Then use it once. The other three instances of the same phrase become something else entirely.

**Weakness 2 — Anna's wry register is absent in 5 of 6 scenes.**
The chapter reads as a competent naval log. The "I had a thought I did not write down" line is the most Anna moment in the chapter and it passes without the parenthetical aside that would make it Anna's voice rather than naval minimalism.

**Fix:** Add one wry parenthetical per major scene. The structural approach is already there in each scene — the Diego standing observation, the Joel Edison paperback, the Priya exit — but the actual wry observation is missing in each case. For the "no Surface 3" beat: "(Which is the kind of thing you know, abstractly, from departure day, and which the body nonetheless insists on re-knowing at the moment it becomes final.)" This is seven seconds of audiobook time and it transforms the line.

**Weakness 3 — The Joel-submarine scene delivers its emotional weight in the wrong syntactic position.**
"He had said the boat had been a submarine called Sunfish. He had said the submarine had been decommissioned in 1998." These two sentences are buried in the middle of an eight-sentence block about what Joel said at abridged tempo. The reader absorbs them as report. The disclosure that the architecture is named after a decommissioned submarine Joel served on — this is a paragraph-closing beat, not a mid-block detail.

**Fix:** Move the Sunfish disclosure to the syntactic close of its paragraph. Cut the surrounding explanation ("He had said the rest at the abridged tempo. The rest had been at the abridged tempo because..."). End on: "He had said the architecture had been named after the boat. He had said the boat had been a submarine called Sunfish. He had said the submarine had been decommissioned in 1998. Then he had finished his tea." Let the reader do the rest.

## Single Highest-Leverage Edit

**Add the parenthetical to the "no Surface 3" realization.**

Current (paras 56-60):

> I had a thought I did not write down.
> 
> And now everything we record for the next ten days is on the boat alone until we surface in Punta Arenas.
> 
> The thought was the thought. The thought arrived at the level the thought arrived at when the body had been on the boat for thirty-eight days...

The structural instinct is correct. The prose execution prevents it from landing.

Rewrite target:

> I had a thought I did not write down. I will write it here.
> 
> *And now everything we record for the next ten days is on the boat alone until we surface in Punta Arenas.*
> 
> (Which is the kind of thing you know from departure day. The manifest says fifty-six days. The timeline says three surface windows. Basic arithmetic. And then you are standing at the command station on Day 39 at 0611 and the body does the arithmetic again and the arithmetic is the same and the answer arrives at a different altitude than it did in September.)
> 
> I returned to the indications.

This single change is the chapter's emotional fulcrum. It takes forty words. It does not require restructuring any other scene. And it installs Anna's voice — parenthetical-aside engine, self-aware emotional compression, body-as-separate-narrator, forward-motion close — at the point in the chapter where the reader most needs to meet her.
