## Stage 1: The Plan (UPF Core Sections)

### 1. Context & Why

Ch03 is a structural load-bearing chapter with no single dramatic scene: it must establish six crew members at operational depth, install the attestation mechanic as an architectural claim, and get the boat under the ice by the close. The evaluation question is: does it do this while functioning as readable, satisfying fiction — not just competent plot plumbing?

### 2. Success Criteria

**PASS conditions:**
- Each crew scene lands with character specificity, not interchangeable exposition
- The Wanjiru attestation scene carries the architectural claim without becoming a lecture
- The chapter opens and closes with Anna's voice fully in register
- Foreshadowing seeds (Priya fourth pass, Diego Day 28, Joel rehearsal) are visible but not telegraphed
- Paragraph-level prose passes the ANNA-VOICE reading test

**FAILED conditions (kill triggers):**
- Voice slippage into Janeway-rhetorical (aphorism closures, parallel tricolons, anaphoric inventories)
- Architectural exposition displacing narrative
- Any crew scene interchangeable with another crew member
- Closing section feels like a checklist rather than a scene

### 4. Phases — Scene-by-Scene Grading

**Scene 1: Drake crossing (opening three paragraphs)**

GRADE: **B+**

Strengths: "the Drake did not behave. It was not built to behave. The boat was built to take what the passage gave it and continue south. That was the contract." — clean, muscular, Anna register. "The Drake's lexicon meant nothing the boat itself had not been built to absorb" — precise compression. The Day 3 bridge-officer wind reading ("He told me with the flatness watch officers use for weather they do not intend to characterize") — very strong character-through-observation move.

Weakness: opening sentence "I came up through the Strait first as a junior, then as a chief scientist, and now as the woman in command" is slightly effortful on first read — the "now as the woman in command" qualifier signals that being a woman in command is a notable category, which ANNA-VOICE warns against (she's past that). The sentence works structurally but has a faint self-consciousness the rest of the chapter does not carry.

---

**Scene 2: Sabina — The Paper and the Network**

GRADE: **A-**

Strengths: The 1998 dock-at-Rio-Grande backstory is the chapter's single best executed piece of character work. "The paper was not theatre. It was the practice of a woman who had been at a dock in 1998." — strong concrete close, ANNA-VOICE compliant. The class/politics secondary rail is present but never labored: Sabina's "the consortium will want the audit checkpoint to be clean" without editorializing, followed by Anna noting the value of logistics officers who don't editorialize, lands the institutional-politics subtext at precisely the right altitude.

Weakness: the transition paragraph starting "I have served with logistics officers who editorialized..." is one of the most Anna-like lines in the chapter, but the close ("Sabina did the work. I logged it. I returned to the deck.") is slightly abrupt — the three-beat cadence has faint parallel-structure tricolon energy that ANNA-VOICE flags. It is not technically an anti-pattern violation (the three beats are not rhythm-tricolons, they are functional actions) but it reads slightly constructed on first listen.

---

**Scene 3: Diego — The List at Low Altitude**

GRADE: **A**

Strengths: This is the chapter's strongest crew scene. The four-boat history (Weddell 2014, Norwegian logistics vessel 2019, Otago 2022 + the transducer story) is character biography done in narrative action, not backstory paragraph. The Otago anecdote — "he let me find it in the post-mission archive on my own" — is a compressed character-revealing scene that earns the Day 28 plant explicitly without telegraphing what Day 28 will be. "The performing is for the consortium meetings" closes the scene on a wry observation that also serves the class/region secondary rail.

The bridge-wing setting (thermos, folded chart with its own topography) is sensory and specific.

The Diego favor ("Diego was bringing it up first... the way a senior officer does a small favor for a junior officer who would otherwise have to do an awkward thing") — good; Anna noticing the favor without performing commentary is pure Bobiverse register.

Weakness: essentially none. This is the chapter's model crew scene. The only note is that "three previous boats" is stated directly three lines before the four-boat enumeration — minor redundancy that a copyedit would catch.

---

**Scene 4: Hiroshi — The Absence of Obstruction**

GRADE: **B**

Strengths: Short and earned. "Hiroshi requires the absence of obstruction" is a precise character-defining sentence. "The calmest man on the manifest had set the calmest watch I had seen since the boat left the pier" — clean summary close. The comparison to anxious chief scientists Anna has sailed with reinforces the "how people respond to Anna" beat (Hiroshi's ease is a posture response to Anna's command style) without naming it.

Weakness: this is the chapter's thinnest scene, and appropriately so — Hiroshi does less at this stage, the scene earns its brevity. But "Hiroshi's calm is contagious in the way other officers' anxiety is contagious" is the chapter's closest brush with an aphoristic close. It is a general truth — nearly packaged. The parallel structure (calm as contagious / anxiety as contagious) is the anti-pattern shape. One small rephrase would close the gap: the "contagious" comparison works; the exact symmetry of the sentence is what reads constructed.

---

**Scene 5: Wanjiru — Attestation Before Partition**

GRADE: **B+** overall; section close is the chapter's highest-risk zone

Strengths: the attestation exchange itself — the brief dialogue, Anna's "acknowledged," the hum resuming, going below — is faultless. The technical payoff paragraph ("what the exchange had been, I logged afterward... installing a baseline") is well-calibrated compression. "Wanjiru attested. The attestation was procedure, not theatre." — strong.

Weakness: the five-paragraph close starting "I want to name what that means..." is the chapter's most extended architectural exposition block. It is the chapter's most direct departure from show-don't-tell. The paragraph that begins "An attestation is what you say when you cannot afford for the next person to misunderstand the state of the world" and closes "That is what Wanjiru is. That is why I had picked them both" — is doing necessary architectural work but is also the passage where Anna comes closest to lecturing. The final sentence "That is what the architecture is. That is what Wanjiru is. That is why I had picked them both" is a three-beat tricolon close — technically a parallel-structure tricolon, which ANNA-VOICE anti-pattern #4 flags. It does not have the rhythm-cycling quality of the flagged examples, but the structural symmetry is present.

This is the scene most at risk of an attentive reader noting "the architecture is being explained to me" rather than experienced through action. The exposition is earned by what precedes it; the close is the specific vulnerability.

---

**Scene 6: Priya — The Fourth Pass**

GRADE: **A-**

Strengths: The best-paced scene in the chapter. The calibration cycle rendered in physical detail (audible weight of the cable, shoulders set, jaw tight, counting partly aloud and partly under breath) is the multi-sensory specificity the literary rubric asks for. The Day 5 instrumentation lab is rendered more specifically than any other location in the chapter. The "she did not negotiate" beat is character-defining without commentary.

The direct-reader address paragraph ("You will hear, in the months that come, a great deal about who failed this mission...") is one of the chapter's best uses of the Bobiverse-direct register: it advances the novel's central credibility-war theme while staying in character.

"The naming is for the staff history — the document where I get to do the naming the watch log does not. You are the reason it exists." — strong. Faint echo of the ANNA-VOICE anti-pattern #6 (more than one staff-history meta-frame per chapter) since the chapter opens with the staff-history frame in a lighter key — but the two instances are at opposite ends of the chapter and serve different functions (opening = orienting register; close = direct reader pay-off), so this is within the "pick the moment where the frame is most load-bearing" guidance.

Weakness: "The discipline took both" (close of the cycle paragraph) — this is the chapter's second "packaged truth" close. It is brief and not an aphorism, but three instances of "the discipline" in the final Priya section (line 155, 157, 158) create the motif-repetition pattern ANNA-VOICE anti-pattern #5 caps at one per chapter. "The discipline took both" / "The discipline did not lift for him, or for the Mission Director either" / "The discipline was where it belonged" — three uses in close proximity. Two should be varied.

---

**Scene 7: Joel — Recorded Fact, Not Memory**

GRADE: **B+**

Strengths: The rehearsal rationale dialogue is clean — not too much, not too little. "A recorded fact rather than a memory of one" is the scene's cargo and it is well-delivered. The callback to ch02 ("if you have read the earlier chapter") is handled without jarring the prose — Bobiverse-direct register at work.

"You are watching it now in its operational register" — slightly at risk of ANNA-VOICE anti-pattern #9 (register overuse), but this is the chapter's only use of "register" in the register-as-bearing sense; the chapter's other "register" instances are bearing/working-register usages (Sabina, Joel) that are also within the ≤3/chapter cap. No violation, but worth confirming in a galley run.

Weakness: the post-rehearsal confirmation paragraph ("The rehearsal ran... the comparison was now a recorded fact") is the flattest prose in the chapter — it serves the plot but does not serve the voice. It is the only passage that reads like a beat sheet rather than narration.

---

**Scene 8: The Edge — Day 7**

GRADE: **A-**

Strengths: The ice-edge arrival is understated and earned. Diego's "the edge is where I said it was" — no performance — is the chapter's quietest payoff. The Diana/Liteyny Prospekt interlude is the chapter's load-bearing emotional beat: "The marginal ice zone at the southernmost edge of the world's southernmost continent at four in the morning on Day 7 of a fifty-six day mission was the place I was actually standing, and the bakery on Liteyny Prospekt at three in the afternoon was the place I was simultaneously, against my discipline, also standing." — the "against my discipline" parenthetical is exactly right.

"I have not yet decided which of the two places is the harder one to leave" — strong, no packaging.

The postcard-not-yet-written image is sensory and pays off the Diana plant.

The penultimate paragraph — the "I will tell you what the surface looked like on Day 7" direct-address close — is the chapter's most ambitious passage and mostly lands. "This is what it looked like on the surface, on the morning the boat was about to leave the surface for the first time, and I want you to remember it." — the repetition of "surface" in two consecutive clauses is slightly inelegant but serves the thematic point (the surface is what she is leaving). The eleven-item inventory that precedes this sentence is at risk of being read as a checklist — it is thematic rather than procedural, but its list structure is exactly the kind of passage that sounds tighter in audiobook delivery than it reads on the page.

Weakness: "I went below" as a chapter close is correct (ANNA-VOICE paragraph-ending list includes "a short sentence that doesn't try to mean too much") but the three-sentence close — "I went below" — after the extended final inventory is slightly anticlimactic given the weight of what preceded it. The inventory earns more close than it gets.

## Top 3 Strengths (cited)

**1. The Diego sequence is the chapter's best character work.**
The bridge-wing scene (thermos, chart with its own topography, "the routing was drawn in Geneva") combined with the four-boat backstory and the Otago transducer anecdote is the chapter's cleanest execution of ANNA-VOICE's character-through-specific-action principle. "He let me find it in the post-mission archive on my own. When I asked him about it on the dock at Lyttelton, he said: the file was where the file should have been. The decision was at the planning meeting." — character-defining without a summary statement, and the Day 28 plant lands without telegraphing.

**2. The Priya fourth-pass scene has the chapter's best scene-level pacing and most commercially strong payoff.**
The physical rendering of the calibration cycle (audible cable weight, counting under breath, shoulders set, jaw tight) is the chapter's best multi-sensory prose. The direct-reader address ("You will hear, in the months that come, a great deal about who failed this mission...") is both in voice and advances the novel's central credibility-war theme. The "naming is for the staff history — the document where I get to do the naming the watch log does not. You are the reason it exists." — one of the volume's most effective direct-reader beats.

**3. The Diana/Liteyny Prospekt interlude is the chapter's strongest emotional beat and its highest-leverage character moment.**
"Against my discipline, also standing" is the right compression. "Diana would say neither — Diana would say that the moon is the same in both places, which is the answer of a child who has not yet had to choose." — this single sentence advances Diana's character, Anna's relationship with her, and the mother wound's downstream effect (Anna is not passing on the wound; she can see the difference between Diana's innocence and her own earned difficulty) without stating any of it. This is the chapter's most technically accomplished passage.

## Top 3 Weaknesses (cited + actionable fixes)

**1. The Wanjiru close over-explains the architectural claim.**
The five-paragraph block starting "I want to name what that means..." ends on "That is what the architecture is. That is what Wanjiru is. That is why I had picked them both" — a tricolon close that ANNA-VOICE anti-pattern #4 flags. The action (the attestation exchange) already delivers the architectural claim; the exposition that follows it is partially redundant. The close's symmetrical structure reads constructed.

**Fix:** Cut the final "That is what the architecture is. That is what Wanjiru is. That is why I had picked them both." Replace with a concrete physical close — something Wanjiru does or that Anna observes in the comm space. The preceding paragraph ("The architecture's discipline was Wanjiru's discipline made portable") is a strong enough close if the tricolon sentence is removed. Saves ~50 words and removes the anti-pattern violation without losing the architectural claim.

**2. "The discipline" appears three times in close proximity in the Priya scene close.**
ANNA-VOICE anti-pattern #5 caps motif phrases at one instance per chapter. Lines 155, 157, and 158 each use "the discipline" in adjacent paragraphs: "The discipline took both" / "The discipline did not lift for him, or for the Mission Director either" / "The discipline was where it belonged."

**Fix:** Keep "The discipline was where it belonged" (strongest instance, chapter close of the scene). Vary the other two. Line 155: "Both of them took it" or similar. Line 157: "The work didn't lift for him" or "She didn't come up for him, or for me."

**3. The post-rehearsal confirmation paragraph (Joel scene) is the chapter's flattest prose.**
"The rehearsal ran on the afternoon watch on Day 6... The comparison was now a recorded fact. I said: acknowledged. He went below." — this passage serves the plot but does not serve the voice. It reads like stage direction. At ~120 words it is not long but it follows one of the chapter's best observational paragraphs and creates a brief trough.

**Fix:** Compress to one sentence ("The rehearsal held. Joel briefed me at sixteen-forty and told me the comparison was now a recorded fact.") and let the prior paragraph carry the weight. The "recorded fact" callback lands without the procedural detail. Save the Day 6 granularity for the watch log, not the staff history.

## Single Highest-Leverage Edit

**Cut the Wanjiru tricolon close and trim the "discipline" motif repetition in the Priya scene.**

These are the chapter's two anti-pattern violations. Both are small fixes (combined ~80 words of revision, no new prose required). The Wanjiru close fix removes the chapter's most visible constructed-voice signal. The discipline-motif fix removes the only line in the chapter that a careful reader might mark as a verbal tic rather than a character observation. Together they push the chapter from B+ to A- on the literary rubric without changing structure, word count, or any planted foreshadowing element.
