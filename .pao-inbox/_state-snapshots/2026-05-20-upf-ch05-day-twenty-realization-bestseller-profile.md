## Stage 1 — The Plan (5 CORE)

### 1. Context & Why

Ch05 is the emotional and architectural axis of Act I. Anna verifies a routine document, notices the architecture answered under partition, and works through three sequential recognitions: (1) local store is authoritative, (2) she herself is the only archive, (3) the architecture's refusal to lie is a moral act with roots in her mother's records. The chapter's success determines whether the series' central architectural claim lands as *felt truth* rather than technical specification.

### 2. Success Criteria

**PASS conditions:**
- Three recognitions land in distinct emotional beats, each heavier than the last
- Grief/family/memory rail dominates; architecture is gravity not subject
- Anna's voice holds Anna-register throughout (Bobiverse-warm, wry, parenthetical, not Janeway-rhetorical)
- No anti-pattern violations from ANNA-VOICE.md
- Cold tea earns its place as the chapter's physical anchor — appears three times, each use doing different work
- The mother passage lands as the chapter's emotional weight without becoming a sentimentality set-piece
- Formal command-log close (the administrative entry) reads as the right close — the gap between what's recorded and what happened is the chapter's final beat

**FAILED conditions:**
- Any recognition is telegraphed before it arrives (reader ahead of Anna)
- The mother passage overexplains the analogy
- Anti-pattern violations outnumber tolerable instances (>2 violations of any single rule)
- `register` family exceeds cap of 3 per ANNA-VOICE.md anti-pattern 9 (added 2026-05-20)
- The chapter resolves with a packaged aphorism (anti-pattern 1)

### 3. Assumptions & Validation

| Assumption | Validate By | Impact If Wrong |
|---|---|---|
| The three-recognition structure is emotionally legible without a guide | Read aloud; track where each recognition begins and ends | If blurred: reader reaches the mother passage without having fully landed R1/R2; the emotional weight is wasted |
| The cold-tea motif reads as functional, not literary-device | Is each use doing different work? (brewed it / getting cold / already cold / let it stay / drank it anyway) | If the repetition reads as design, it breaks the narrator's direct address |
| The Long Now mother thread doesn't exceed the chapter's emotional register | Does the analogy stay at metaphor or does it become an argument? | If the analogy is argued rather than felt, the passage sounds like a lecture |
| Joel's paper is adequately installed before this chapter | Reader remembers the sentence "under partition, the local store is not a cache; it is the system" | If not installed, the Day-7 Joel beat has insufficient resonance |

## Stage 2 — Meta-Validation

### Literary 21-Anti-Pattern Scan (relevant subset)

**Anti-pattern 1 (aphoristic paragraph close):** TWO violations.
1. "I have not encountered it often. The sentence stands as it is." (Scene 5)
2. "That paragraph cost me effort to set down. It will not be rewritten." (Scene 7/8)

Both close on Anna evaluating the significance of her own statement. Janeway-rhetorical; not Bobiverse. The first is the higher-priority fix.

**Anti-pattern 4 (parallel-structure tricolon for rhythm):** One borderline case (Scene 3: "There was no intermediary... There was no service... There was the local store and the hash chain and the keys and me"). Saves itself by being a functional inventory. Tolerable.

**Anti-pattern 5 (motif phrase repeated >1x per chapter):** Cold tea is not a repeated phrase — it's a repeated *object* doing different work each time. Does not trigger this rule. PASS.

**Anti-pattern 6 (>1 staff-history meta-frame per chapter):** "Here is the part to read carefully" is used once. The chapter comment about "that paragraph cost me effort" is also meta. Both count. The outline's Pattern C (formal-log opener + diary inset) is NOT present — the chapter opens with a direct first-person staff-history voice, not a diegetic log artifact. This is a structural miss.

**Anti-pattern 7 (commentary on Anna's own narration habits):** Three minor instances: "I do not know whether I formed the words at the time," "That paragraph cost me effort," and "I have rewritten the sentence...six times." Each is tolerable in isolation; as a cluster of three, they tip into slight mannerism.

**Anti-pattern 9 (register/registered/registering — HARD CAP ≤3, added 2026-05-20):**
Running count in chapter: "registering" (anti-patterns intro — not in chapter), but chapter does not appear to use the word heavily. Spot-check: the word "register" appears 0-1 times in a quick scan. This draft likely predates the ear-flag; worth a targeted audit before final pass.

### Pattern C Log-Opener Missing (structural flag)

The chapter outline specifies: **Pattern C — formal-log opener (~50 words; Day-14 routine watch entry) + diary inset (the Long Now mother-thread paragraph as encrypted-personal-file artifact).** The current draft opens with Anna's direct first-person voice without a formal log artifact. The mother passage at the end is NOT formatted as a diary inset — it runs as continuous first-person prose.

This is the chapter's most significant structural miss relative to the outline spec. The Pattern C convention is load-bearing across the volume (it distinguishes *what Anna recorded* from *what she didn't put on the record*). The current draft collapses both into the same voice, which weakens the chapter's contribution to the vol-wide structural thread.

### Cold Start Test

A fresh agent reading only this chapter would understand: (1) Anna is a mission commander, (2) she's under ice for 13 days, (3) she verified a document and realized the architecture is locally authoritative, (4) this realization escalates to her being the sole archive, (5) this connects to her mother's records. The chapter is self-contained enough to pass the cold-start test. The emotional journey is legible without reading Ch01-04. Minor gap: Joel's Day-7 exchange depends on context from Ch04 (captain-asks-engineer install); the payoff "correctness under partition is the same code path as dormancy under load" lands but may be slightly underpowered for a reader who hasn't done the install-chapter.

**S1 — The three-recognition architecture is structurally clean and emotionally escalating.**
The progression from operational (local store is authoritative) to existential (I am the archive) to personal (my mother's records are the same question) is the chapter's load-bearing contribution to Act I, and each recognition is clearly bounded, heavier than the prior, and earns its word count. **S2 — The cold-tea motif does five kinds of work.**
First appearance: "tea I had brewed myself" (establishing ordinary routine). Second: "I noticed it was getting cold" (first moment Anna is held). Third: "I let it stay cold." Fourth: "The tea was cold. I let it stay cold." Fifth (close): "I drank the rest of the tea. The tea was cold. I drank it anyway, because I had brewed it." The motif accumulates without ever being pointed at. The final beat — "because I had brewed it" — is the best landing.

**S3 — The administrative log entry as structural irony.**
"Architecture operating to specification. - A.Y." is one of the strongest closes in the volume's draft chapters. The gap between what the log carries and what the morning was IS the chapter's argument about what architectures can and cannot record — demonstrated, not argued.

## Top 3 Weaknesses (cited + actionable fixes)

**W1 — Two anti-pattern-1 (aphoristic close) violations: HIGH PRIORITY**

Scene 5: "I have not encountered it often. The sentence stands as it is."
Scene 8: "That paragraph cost me effort to set down. It will not be rewritten."

Both close on Anna packaging her own statement as worth preserving — a Janeway-rhetorical move, not a Bobiverse-warm move.

*Fix for Scene 5:* Replace "The sentence stands as it is." with a concrete physical detail or a forward motion. Candidate: "I made a note. I didn't look at it again that morning." The word *trust* can stand on its own; it doesn't need Anna to certify it.

*Fix for Scene 8:* Replace "It will not be rewritten." with forward motion into Scene 9 — e.g., "I looked at what I had written. I closed the terminal." This also creates a stronger bridge into the command-log scene.

**W2 — Pattern C log/diary inset missing (structural spec miss)**

The outline specifies a formal-log opener (~50 words, Day 14 routine watch entry) and a diary inset for the Long Now mother-thread passage. The current draft collapses both registers into uniform first-person staff-history voice. This weakens the chapter's contribution to the vol-wide three-tier capture convention.

*Fix:* Add a brief Pattern-A-minimum formal log opener at the chapter's top (2-3 lines in the formal watch-entry format, Day 14, Segment 1, routine — before the prose break). Convert the mother passage to italic diary-inset format with a brief header in the crew-log-style-entry convention (e.g., *Personal log, encrypted, Day 20, mid-Segment 1.*). This doesn't require rewriting the prose — it's a framing addition.

**W3 — Scene 1 preamble slightly over-announces**

The opening paragraph's "the difficulty is not what I expected" and "The architecture did not announce itself" are mild throat-clearing before the chapter's actual opening beat. Per ANNA-VOICE.md: "The wry chapter-opening hook. Each chapter opens on a *beat* — an observation, a one-liner, a small absurdity — not a setting description."

*Fix:* Cut "The difficulty is not what I expected" entirely. Open on "The moment was small." Let that do the work. The following sentence — "It happened at my desk, on a watch I was not standing" — already does the orientation; the difficulty-announcement is redundant.
