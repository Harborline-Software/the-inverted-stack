The book's premise (architecture-as-character, technical-thriller-with-mother-wound, polar-research-as-institutional-contest) is **commercially atypical** — it is closer to Robert Caro's *Path to Power* in its institutional-history texture than to Bobiverse's adventure-cadence, even though the prose register targets Bobiverse. **There is genuine commercial risk** that an agent reads ch05 (the recommended sample) and recognizes the craft but doesn't see how to pitch the book to an editor whose acquisition target is "techno-thriller for hard-SF readers."

**This is not a craft problem. Whether it sells at A+ is downstream of choices PAO doesn't control: cover art, jacket copy, agent fit, editor fit, comparable-titles pitching, the marketing narrative the publisher constructs around it.

### Cold-Start Test: does the volume work for a reader who hasn't read vol-1?

Vol-1 does not exist. Vol-2 IS the first written volume in the series — it was rebranded mid-development. The Cold-Start Test for this volume is **whether a reader can enter the series at vol-2 without prior context**, and the answer is **yes**:

The volume **passes the cold-start test**. A reader with no series knowledge can begin at ch01 and finish at ch18 with full comprehension.
