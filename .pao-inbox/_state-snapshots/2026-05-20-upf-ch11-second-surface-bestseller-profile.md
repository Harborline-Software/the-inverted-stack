## PAO Summary Report (≤400 words)

### Top 3 Strengths (cited)

**1. Wanjiru's institutional weight lands correctly.**
The outline's load-bearing instruction is that Ch 11 is "where Wanjiru's institutional weight begins forming on the page." The draft delivers: she elevates the Stefan cross-check at 0540, *before* Anna arrives, *before* being asked. The "two pages, no accusation, offering of evidence" framing she presents is not procedural compliance — it is a four-year argument arriving at its first evidentiary instance. The "She would say none of these things in the wardroom now" paragraph (lines 113-117) is the chapter's single strongest passage: it performs the institutional weight by naming what Wanjiru is *not* saying and trusting the reader to feel the weight of the omission.

**2. Joel's cup scene is structurally correct.**
The cup-of-tea beat (lines 137-143) correctly follows ANNA-VOICE.md's instruction: "One specific Joel-observation per chapter where he appears. Concrete, never abstract." The cup at the elbow, the tea at the temperature he knew she drank it at, the reading posture — these are the right details. The scene's mechanics are right.

**3. Foreshadowing is clean and disciplined.**
Priya's "Three passes. Clear." (line 77) is a two-word plant for Ch 13 that earns its place without announcing itself. The diary-entry access-control paragraph (lines 174-176) plants the conditional-preservation stake that Ch 14 needs. Both plants-set from the chapter header land in the draft.

### Top 3 Weaknesses (cited + actionable fixes)

**1. The Joel cup scene runs too long (lines 138-143).**
Anna's interior on the sixteen-day tracking pattern is currently ~250 words — almost a full page on a beat the outline describes as "a quality of her attention, not a beat of narrative motion." The passage narrates the emotional recognition at a level of explicitness that the ANNA-VOICE spec calls out as anti-pattern #7 (no commentary on Anna's own narration habits). It also risks anti-pattern #8 (echo-and-confirm) in the "body-level register — slower than the architecture..." construction.
**Fix:** Compress the interior from ~250 words to ≤80 words. Keep: the cup was there, the elbow detail, that she had been tracking this since Day 22. Cut: the architectural-metaphor framing ("slower than the architecture caught the partition transition"), the inventory of the three preceding pattern instances (wardroom seat, evening rotation, morning coffee). The reader has been registering what Anna has been registering; she does not need to enumerate it.

**2. The chapter title's "Second Forsaken Reveal" is not delivered in the body.**
The Forsaken position glossary flags this specifically: "consider whether Vol 2 should add a brief in-universe gloss the first time Forsaken appears in chapter prose." Ch 11 is the *second* named reveal and the Forsaken framing — the rival narrative being mounted on the surface — never appears by name or in Anna's interior. The chapter delivers the evidentiary counter-move (the cross-check) but not the political framing that names what they are countering.
**Fix:** One sentence in Anna's interior at the comm-space door (lines 165-167, the standing-at-the-door beat) naming what Wanjiru's two pages are countering: the institutional narrative that local-first architecture is what the next mission should leave behind. Four words would do it: *The Forsaken position. Filed against.* Or equivalent.

**3. Multi-sense grounding is almost absent.**
The chapter's physical environment — the comm space, the wardroom, the bridge — is reported at the level of location labels. "The hum was on" is the only recurring sensory anchor. At audiobook-native design, listeners need something to hear, smell, or feel that grounds them in the boat between technical exchanges.
**Fix:** Two targeted sensory insertions. (a) At the scene 1 bridge-wing beat (line 33): one physical detail beyond the wind — temperature, the ice smell, the angle of the sun already higher than thirty-seven days ago. (b) At the wardroom tea scene (line 137): the metal cup's specific weight or warmth in Anna's hand. Neither needs more than one sentence.
