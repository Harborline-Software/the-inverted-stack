# Mission Log - Day 28
## the Nansen, Mid-Segment 2

---

The mesh held through the second surfacing. We came up at 0419 local time on Day 23 and were back under the ice by 0731 on Day 25, ahead of the weather window the consortium had advised us to clear. The reconciliation took forty-six minutes; Wanjiru ran it; I watched. The audit trail closed clean. The architecture did what Joel said it would do, in roughly the time he said it would take, with the kind of margin that I am not yet ready to call comfortable.

I will say this once and not return to it: the second surfacing was harder than the first. The first surfacing was a relief - we had proved the architecture survives a fourteen-day partition, we were where we expected to be on the chart, the science was running. The second surfacing arrived with the news that Stefan's mission had concluded, that the public framing of his platform's success was already on the record, and that the consortium's institutional supporters had begun briefing legislators on a federated-trust transition framework. We surfaced into a smaller Pattern than the one we had left.

This is what I had not understood about partition before this voyage. Partition is not silence. Partition is a kind of geological time. The world above moves; you move on a different clock. When the clocks reconcile, the world has shifted in ways your local store cannot track. Joel's architecture handles state; it does not handle the political weather. Nothing handles the political weather. We will have to do that ourselves.

I have written separate notes on Joel's behavior during the surfacing window. He was where he needed to be. He was correct in every operational decision. He worked the reconciliation alongside Wanjiru without performing competence, without seeking acknowledgment, with the discipline that I have come to recognize as the version of his Navy training that survived his civilian transition. I will not say more here. I have written the rest in a private file the staff history will not include.

The crew is well. Diego's barometer-watch instinct says the weather window into Segment 3 is shorter than the consortium's model predicted; we will move at 0530 tomorrow rather than at 0700 as planned. I have agreed. The mission is the work that arrives, not the work that was scheduled. Sabina says the same in Bangla. Wanjiru hums.

Mid-Segment 2. Eleven days to mission Day 39. We dive again at dawn.

*- A. Y., Day 28, the Nansen*
