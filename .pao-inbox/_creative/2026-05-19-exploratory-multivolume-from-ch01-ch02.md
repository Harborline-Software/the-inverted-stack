# Exploratory Multi-Volume Series Shapes — From Vol-2 Ch01 + Ch02 Alone

## Methodology + constraint

A reader who has read only ch01 (Departure) and ch02 (The Recruitment Interview) carries a specific set of narrative gravity wells. This document asks: **what series would a writer who had read only those two chapters naturally extrapolate?**

The exercise is exploratory. It is NOT a recommendation to abandon the current trajectory canon. It IS a structural stress-test that reveals which trajectory choices were inevitable from ch01+ch02 and which were creative bets that could have gone another direction.

## The four natural multi-volume series shapes that emerge

A writer extrapolating from ch01+ch02 alone would most naturally land on one of four series shapes. Each pursues a different gravity well from the chapters.

---

### Shape A — "Anna's Career Arc" (3-5 volumes; the natural extrapolation)

**Premise:** Anna's late-career mission cycle. Vol-1 = MERIDIAN-7. Vol-2+ = the missions that follow, the institutional fallout, the family that aged with her.

**Per-volume natural extrapolation:**

| Vol | Setting | Central arc | Plant payoffs |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 2026, the *Nansen* under ice (96 days) | The mission + the dual-betrayal cascade + Joel-Anna resolution + the firmware unease detonates | 93-day Joel counter; Wardroom 11:30; firmware smallest-feeling; "three others" |
| 2 | 2027, post-surface aftermath | Credibility war + Anna's mother dies + Anna writes the staff history Diana will one day read; new mission begins to materialize | Mother-wound thread; staff-history-as-Anna's-legacy; Diana-postcard arc deepens |
| 3 | 2029-2030, second mission | Different boat, partly different crew. Architecture has matured; new failure modes. Diana is 9, beginning to ask the questions; the prior-mission shadow recurs | Architecture-second-deployment; prior-architect shadow finally named; Diana's becoming-her-own-person |
| 4 (optional) | 2032+, handover | Anna passes command to someone she trained. The boat-that-was-the-Nansen retires or is renamed. Diana is 11-12, reads Anna's staff history for the first time | Anna-aging-out; institutional-continuity through-people-not-protocols; Diana receives the record |
| 5 (optional, far future) | 2040s, Diana adulthood | Diana is in her early 20s; works in a related field; engages with what Anna left behind | Multi-generational architecture-as-record |

**Series shape:** intimate; family-and-career-anchored; ~4-5 volumes over ~10-15 years of fictional time; concludes within Anna's lifetime + Diana's coming-of-age. ~400-500k words total. ~50 hours audiobook total.

**Genre positioning:** literary hard-SF / late-career-professional drama / multi-generational family arc with technical substrate. Closer to Wallace Stegner's *Crossing to Safety* + KSR's *Aurora* than to Bobiverse.

**What this shape OPTIMIZES:** the mother-wound character engine; the Joel-Anna relationship; the Diana-scrapbook-postcard thread; Anna's voice as career-spanning narrator.

**What this shape DEFERS:** the architecture's centuries-long persistence question; the institutional-builder Wanjiru arc; the Federation-era destination.

---

### Shape B — "The Prior Mission" (2-3 volumes; flashback-led)

**Premise:** Vol-1 narrates MERIDIAN-7 (the current setting). Vol-2 is set 5 years earlier and narrates the failed prior mission — the one whose architect hid the schema-migration limitation and ended Anna's confidence in the procurement process. The "prior architect" gets a name, a face, a defense; Anna's after-action-report-as-lie becomes load-bearing.

**Per-volume natural extrapolation:**

| Vol | Setting | Central arc |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 2026, MERIDIAN-7 | The current mission |
| 2 | 2021-2022 (flashback), the prior mission | The architect who failed — full POV; the operational-window loss; the after-action report Anna wrote that protected him; the five years between then and now |
| 3 | 2027-2028, aftermath of both | The credibility war + the prior architect resurfacing publicly + the moral cost of Anna's protective after-action report being audited by the consortium |

**Series shape:** tight; trauma-resolution-anchored; ~3 volumes. ~300k words total. ~32 hours audiobook total.

**Genre positioning:** technical thriller / professional-moral-reckoning / institutional-investigation. Closer to *Daemon* (Suarez) + *The Light Between Oceans* than to Bobiverse.

**What this shape OPTIMIZES:** the prior-architect shadow named in ch02 (the four-sentences setup); Anna's moral architecture; the after-action-report-as-technically-true-operationally-a-lie discipline.

**What this shape DEFERS:** Diana; the second mission; the institutional persistence question.

---

### Shape C — "The Architecture Cycles" (4-6 volumes; architecture-as-protagonist)

**Premise:** Each volume narrates a different deployment of the Sunfish architecture, with different operators. Anna is Vol-1; future operators are Vol-2+. The architecture itself becomes the connecting thread; narrators rotate.

**Per-volume natural extrapolation:**

| Vol | Setting | Narrator | Architecture stress-test |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 2026, *Nansen* under-ice | Anna | First operational deployment; partition-tolerance |
| 2 | 2027-2028, second platform | new narrator — could be Priya (research consortium) or a new figure (relief organization, NGO field deployment) | Different stress: high-cardinality / multi-tenant / regulatory-cross-border |
| 3 | 2030s, third platform | new narrator | Different stress: long-duration storage / archival queries / post-failure analysis |
| 4+ | continued deployments | rotating narrators | The architecture's invariants persist; the operators change |

**Series shape:** architecture-anthology; ~4-6 volumes over ~10-15 years. Each volume can stand alone as a thriller; the architecture-as-substrate connects them.

**Genre positioning:** technical-thriller anthology / architecture-as-protagonist. Closest precedent: Greg Egan's *Diaspora* (architecture-spanning narratives) or William Gibson's *Sprawl* trilogy (technology-spanning narratives).

**What this shape OPTIMIZES:** the architecture-as-substrate discipline; the multi-narrator series shape; the recurring-failure-mode pattern (OSS-funding-asymmetry hinted at by the firmware unease).

**What this shape DEFERS:** Anna's full character arc; the mother-wound resolution; Diana.

---

### Shape D — "Single Long Volume; Series Stops at Vol-1" (1 volume)

**Premise:** Vol-1 stands alone. It is the complete story. The credibility war Anna threatens to wage is unresolved at book close; the reader is left with the staff history Anna wrote and the implication that the war played out off-page. No sequels needed.

**Series shape:** single volume; ~120-150k words; ~13 hours audiobook.

**Genre positioning:** literary thriller / standalone professional drama. Closest precedent: Kazuo Ishiguro's *The Buried Giant* + Daniel Suarez's *Influx* + Edward P. Jones's *The Known World*.

**What this shape OPTIMIZES:** completion. Every plant gets paid within one volume; no cross-volume coordination required; one editorial cycle to ship.

**What this shape DEFERS:** every multi-volume opportunity.

## Comparison to the locked trajectory canon

The current `series-arc-sunfish-trajectory.md` chooses a fifth shape — not naturally extrapolated from ch01+ch02, but plausibly consistent with them:

### Shape T (Trajectory Canon, locked 2026-05-05) — "The Centuries-Long Architecture"

8 books, ~250 years, ending with the Federation-era Computer as Sunfish ten generations evolved. Multi-narrator with rotating perspectives across decades and centuries. The architecture is the through-line; the operators are time-bounded; institutional persistence is the load-bearing claim.

### What the trajectory canon adds beyond ch01+ch02

- The standards-body institution (Wanjiru's centuries-long arc) — NOT in ch01+ch02
- The Federation-Computer destination — NOT in ch01+ch02
- Stefan Reinhardt as recurring villain — NOT in ch01+ch02
- The Forsaken / Helvetia / OSS-funding-asymmetry as institutional thesis — NOT in ch01+ch02
- The multi-narrator rotation (Anna → Wanjiru → Astrid → future) — NOT in ch01+ch02 (though Wanjiru is established as future-institutional-figure inflection in Vol-2 Ch 6 + Ch 15+; not in ch01+ch02)

### What the trajectory canon DOESN'T naturally extrapolate from ch01+ch02

- Anna's mother dying (the mother thread resolves in trajectory only by Anna's natural aging-out; the kitchen-table reading scene's emotional gravity is under-paid)
- Diana's coming-of-age (Diana barely appears past Vol-2; her postcard scrapbook thread doesn't get a centuries-long payoff)
- The Joel-Anna relationship resolution (in trajectory, this resolves within Vol-2 and Joel becomes substrate-laying; the green-mug emotional weight pays once)
- The prior-architect shadow (in trajectory, Stefan recurs as institutional villain; the *specific* moral weight Anna names in ch02 — "I wrote the after-action report that protected him" — gets less than full development)

## The exploratory finding

The trajectory canon chose Shape T. Ch01+ch02 most naturally extrapolate to Shape A (Anna's career arc). The two are NOT incompatible — Shape T uses the same ch01+ch02 setup as Shape A would — but they bet on different gravity wells.

**Shape A bets on:** mother-wound + Diana-thread + Anna-Joel-relationship + Anna-as-career-narrator.

**Shape T bets on:** institutional-persistence + multi-narrator + architecture-as-centuries-substrate + the OSS-funding-asymmetry as recurring villain.

The bet is the strategic choice. The trajectory canon (locked 2026-05-05) commits to T; Shape A is the alternative that the chapters most naturally suggest.

### What this exploratory exercise reveals (no judgment, just observations)

1. **Shape A is "intimate-natural."** It optimizes the emotional engines ch01+ch02 establish (mother, Diana, Joel-Anna). It is the shape a reader of ch01+ch02 would predict.

2. **Shape T is "structurally ambitious."** It bets on a centuries-long arc the chapters don't telegraph. The chapters can support it (they don't contradict it), but they don't predict it either.

3. **Shape B "Prior Mission flashback"** would be the most thriller-genre-native choice. It would make Anna's after-action-report-as-lie the moral spine. It is largely abandoned in the current trajectory (Stefan as institutional shadow only; the prior-mission flashback never happens as full-volume).

4. **Shape C "Architecture Cycles"** is what the trajectory canon partially becomes — but Shape C as natural extrapolation would have Anna recur as narrator + rotating co-narrators, not as a single-volume narrator who is replaced. The trajectory canon's commitment to Anna-only-in-Vol-2 is a stronger structural bet than Shape C requires.

5. **Shape D "Standalone Vol-1"** is the safest commercial bet (one editorial cycle; no cross-volume coordination) but defers every multi-volume opportunity ch01+ch02 surface.

### The single most actionable observation from this exploration

**The current trajectory's commitment to Anna-only-as-Vol-1-narrator is the load-bearing structural choice that distinguishes Shape T from Shape A.** Shape A would have Anna narrate Vol-1, Vol-2 (aftermath), Vol-3 (second mission), and possibly Vol-4 (handover) — four volumes of Anna's voice across her late career. Shape T uses Anna only in Vol-1 and rotates to other narrators.

The exploratory question this surfaces: **does the multi-narrator commitment (locked in Shape T) optimize for the series, or for the trajectory canon's centuries-long ambition?** If for the centuries-long ambition: the multi-narrator commitment is correct. If for the series's commercial-narrative gravity: Shape A's Anna-continues-as-narrator might be a stronger bet, at the cost of the centuries-long ambition.

This is not a recommendation to revisit the trajectory. It is a flag that the multi-narrator commitment is a creative bet, not a structural necessity from ch01+ch02 alone.
