- **Languages:** Portuguese or Spanish native, English fluent (working language of international medicine and polar science).

## Backstory (Ch1 → The Crossing)

In Ch1, Maria was a hospital administrator who lived through a catastrophic data-loss event — patient records inaccessible, the SaaS vendor unreachable, patients facing real consequences. The Ch1 scenario established her as a victim of cloud-architecture failure.

Between Ch1 and The Crossing — over five to seven fictional years — she pivoted careers. She returned to clinical medicine, completed a maritime / polar medicine fellowship, and built a parallel career as a data architect for medical-records systems in resource-constrained environments. The Sunfish-1 mission's offline-first medical records system was designed by her. The consortium recruited her as Medical Officer specifically because she designed it.

Her arc from Ch1 victim to mission authority is the chapter's strongest connected-cast move. The reader meets her as the hospital admin who couldn't access records when it mattered; in The Crossing she is the architect of records that cannot be inaccessible. Full arc, earned, no contrivance.

## Personality / voice

- **Default mode:** Quiet, attentive, professionally warm. Bedside manner native — she was a clinician before she was an administrator.
- **Voice register:** Direct, plain-language, Latin-American-inflected English (the Portuguese or Spanish does not appear in the chapter as code-switching except possibly one or two phrases of stress / exhaustion).
- **Tic — King-required residual scarring (load-bearing detail):** She still checks the medical records data **three times** before every watch handoff. Once would be procedure. Three times is recovery work she has not finished. The tic surfaces in Act II (Director observes it), recurs in Act III (during the bridge-death event Maria checks the records compulsively even as she's running resuscitation), and resurfaces in Act V (Director writes about it in the staff history's closing pages). The reader who read Ch1 sees what the tic costs her. The Director sees it and does not comment on it directly — but writes about it later.

## What she wants

To do her job. To deliver the mission's medical care to the standard the architecture promised. To prove — to herself, mostly — that the work she did between Ch1 and now means the records cannot be lost.

## What she needs

To stop checking three times. (She will not stop, in the chapter. The chapter does not redeem the tic. King's "Maria still scarred" is honored: she remains scarred at the end. Her competence still costs her.)

## What she fears

- Specific clinical fear: someone dying on her watch in the medical bay. This fear *materializes* in Act III when the Senior Technical Specialist dies despite her work.
- Underlying architectural fear: the records becoming inaccessible despite everything she built. This fear *materializes* in Act IV when the relay window closes silent and the medical records do not move.
- She does not name either fear aloud. She lives them.

## Role in each act

- **Act I (Departure):** Introduced through Director's eyes. Brief biography. The reader who read Ch1 recognizes her name. The Director does not narrate Maria's Ch1 backstory in the staff history — the reader brings it.
- **Act II (Submerged Transit):** Daily medical rounds. The three-checks tic is observed by the Director once and noted in the staff history without comment. Maria's competence is shown through procedural details.
- **Act III (The Death — present tense set-piece):** Maria runs the resuscitation attempt. Forty minutes. The Director writes the scene with Maria as the closest-to-the-body presence. Maria does not save him. After he is pronounced, Maria checks the records three times — even while the body is still on the bed. The Director sees this. The reader who read Ch1 understands what Maria is doing.
- **Act IV (Silent Window):** Maria is the second voice the Director carries through the silent window. The medical records are not moving. The Senior Technical Specialist's medical history — the autopsy data, the death certificate Maria has already prepared — is in the queue, waiting for relay. Maria checks the records three times. There is nothing else for her to do.
- **Act V (Return):** Maria closes the medical record formally during the homeward transit. The Director writes about her in the staff history's closing pages — one paragraph. The tic remains.
