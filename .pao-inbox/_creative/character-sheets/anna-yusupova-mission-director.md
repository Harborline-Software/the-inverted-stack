# Character Sheet - Anna Yusupova, Mission Director

## What this sheet is

Anna is Vol 2's first-person narrator and the load-bearing voice across the series. The authoritative spec for how she **sounds** lives in `vol-2/ANNA-VOICE.md` - narrator register, anti-patterns, mother-wound architecture, paragraph-ending rules. When in doubt, the voice spec wins for prose; this sheet wins for character mechanics.

## Quick reference (compressed character core)

- **Role:** Mission Director, RV *Nansen*, MERIDIAN-7 mission (Vol 2). Russian Antarctic Institute (AARI) lead investigator, mid-fifties, twenty years of dissolved-chemistry fieldwork. Prior failed mission five years back is the wound that shaped the recruitment of Joel, Priya, Wanjiru.
- **Voice (one-line):** *Janeway with parentheticals.* First-person past tense; staff-history register lightened; Bobiverse-dominant calibration with Janeway bearing, not Janeway rhetoric.

- **Love language (one-line):** Action, never declaration. She tells crew to eat, sleep, take breaks the same way she gives operational orders.
- **Domestic anchor:** Niece Diana, age four, in Voronezh. Postcards out; crayon drawings back. Once-per-act minimum.
- **Bakery rule (one-line):** Coffee is staple; bakeries are *the love*. Travels four continents organized around them. See **Pastry-critic register** below.

## Pastry-critic register (FULL DETAIL - 2026-05-17 update)

Anna evaluates baked goods the way a competent restaurant critic evaluates a plate. The vocabulary is one of the chief sources of the book's playfulness, and it gets its own register only in bakery scenes - Anna does not bring critic-prose into operational scenes; the contrast is the point.

### Structural framework

A critic breaks a pastry down into four observable axes and closes with a quick overall judgment:

1. **Appearance** - color, structure, layering, sheen, geometry of the bake
2. **Aroma** - what reaches the nose before the first bite (butter, yeast, caramelization, fruit, spice)
3. **Texture** - what the bite does in the mouth (shatter, pull, give, crumb)
4. **Flavor** - what the texture delivers (depth, sweetness balance, richness, finish)
5. **Verdict** - a one-line overall judgment, often counterintuitive or affectionate

**Rule of adjective-stacking.** Two or three specific adjectives beat one vague one. *"Shatteringly crisp, wafer-thin layers"* > *"flaky."* *"Tender, short, leans-cookie crumb"* > *"soft."*

**Rule of physical specificity.** Critique through what the pastry **does**, not how it **feels** in the abstract. *"The layers explode into flakes with each bite"* > *"satisfying texture."*

### Vocabulary bank

#### Texture-focused phrases
- "Shatteringly crisp layers that explode into flakes with each bite."
- "A tender, short crumb that leans more cookie than pastry."
- "Heavy and a little doughy in the center, with a tough, tired crust."
- "Beautiful contrast: brittle shell, pillowy interior, and a chewy pull at the center."

#### Flavor and aroma phrases
- "Rich, clean butter flavor that lingers without feeling greasy."
- "Nicely caramelized edges bring a toasty, nutty depth to the sweetness."
- "The filling is bluntly sweet, drowning out the fruit's natural tartness."
- "A whisper of yeast and ripe wheat in the crumb, like good country bread."

#### Overall verdict phrases
- "Technically impressive but oddly joyless, more showpiece than pleasure."
- "Comforting and rustic, the kind of pastry you finish without realizing."
- "All drama, no follow-through: spectacular layers, forgettable flavor."
- "Flawed but charming; you forgive the soggy base for that gorgeous aroma."

### Anna's specific deployment rules

1. **Never the full four-axis breakdown in one go.** A critic in print might walk all five (Appearance → Aroma → Texture → Flavor → Verdict). Anna in narration picks **one or two** and lands the verdict - usually inside an em-dash insert or a single sentence. The full critic-prose would read as performance; Anna is not performing for the reader, she is registering what she found.
2. **Brevity over completeness.** *"Short crust, the curd set without being grainy, no soggy bottom"* (ch02) - three specifics, one negation, done. Three to five clauses max per bakery moment.
3. **The verdict is dry, not effusive.** Anna does not say *"divine"* or *"transcendent."* She says *"It was correct"* or *"the bake was confident"* or *"two minutes more would have ruined it."* The Janeway-with-parentheticals register holds even in the kitchen.
4. **Negation is a tool.** Anna often praises a pastry by what it **didn't** do: *"the curd set without being grainy"*; *"no soggy bottom"*; *"the proportions were not timid."* Negative-space praise is part of the discipline.
5. **The fork-test is a thing she does.** She will lift the edge of a tart with the side of her fork to check the base while the proprietor is looking the other way. This is in-character and recurs. She has stopped pretending she is sorry about it.
6. **Bakery scenes are the playground; ship scenes are not.** Critic-register stays in pastry contexts. The galley on the *Nansen* gets one rueful sentence per chapter ("the galley is not a bakery; Mikael has been hearing about it for seven years") - not a four-axis critique.

### Retired/forbidden moves

- **~~"What it claimed to be."~~** Retired per ANNA-VOICE.md 2026-05-13. Don't reintroduce as a verdict catchphrase. Anna can evaluate pastry seriously without a signature tic; the vocabulary above does the work.
- **No food-influencer register.** No *"to die for,"* no *"the best I've ever had,"* no *"changed my life,"* no exclamation marks. The critic register is dry; effusion is out-of-character.
- **No metaphor inflation.** *"Buttery hymn"* / *"a symphony of textures"* / *"the dough sang"* - all out. Anna names what is physically true and stops there. The pastry does not sing; the layers shatter.

### Pastry types Anna has informed opinions about

Working roster (extend as scenes require):

- **Vatrushka** - Russian curd-filled bun. Tuesday-only at the St. Petersburg bakery near her office. The ch02 baseline ("short crust, the curd set without being grainy, no soggy bottom") is the register.
- **Kuchen de murta** - Patagonian myrtle-berry kuchen. Appears at the Punta Arenas pier in ch01. Three dozen specific kind, not assortment.
- **Medialunas** - Argentine pastry. Anna has opinions about glaze ratios. Lyon, Buenos Aires, Punta Arenas - all different schools, all judged.
- **Country bread / pain de campagne** - The aroma-and-crumb baseline. *"A whisper of yeast and ripe wheat in the crumb"* is canon.
- **Tarts (open-faced, fruit)** - Where the fork-test happens. Soggy bottom is the canonical failure mode.
- **Croissants and viennoiserie** - Lamination work. *"Shatteringly crisp, wafer-thin layers"* is the praise; *"all drama, no follow-through"* is the put-down.
- **Anything she does not recognize from a window display** - She walks in anyway. The bakery in Lisbon she would have run on a corner near a fountain remains hypothetical.

## Cross-references for prose-drafting

When writing an Anna bakery beat, the chapter-drafter should:
1. Read **`vol-2/ANNA-VOICE.md`** § "The bakery thing" + § "The pastry-judge vocabulary" for the narrator register.
2. Read this sheet's **Pastry-critic register** section for the four-axis framework and the deployment rules (especially: brevity, negation, verdict-as-dry).
3. Pick **one or two** axes for the moment. Never all five. Anna is a character, not a Yelp review.
4. Land the verdict in Anna's voice: dry, often negative-space, never effusive.
