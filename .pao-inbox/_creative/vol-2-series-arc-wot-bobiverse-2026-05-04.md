# Vol 2+ Series Arc - WoT + Bobiverse Hybrid Structure

## CO decision (confirmed this session)

Series direction: WoT + Bobiverse hybrid.
- Each book advances both the immediate story **and** the decades-long contest over information sovereignty
- The Forsaken = regulatory bodies and corporate interests
- The Pattern = the mesh
- Characters drawn from the vol-2-concept-locked-elements locked cast

---

## The cosmological layer (always-on; never explained as exposition)

| WoT concept | Series analog |
|---|---|
| The Pattern | The mesh - self-healing, partition-tolerant, resistant to centralization by design |
| The Dark One | The principle of total information control (not a person; a direction of travel) |
| The Bore | The moment a major regulatory body first successfully compelled cloud disclosure |
| The Breaking | The centralized-SaaS era: user data fully corporate-controlled; the world fractured into dependent fragments |
| The Third Age | Now. The contest is live but not yet decided. |
| The Last Battle | Achieving legislative or architectural lock-in for local-first data sovereignty - whichever side gets there first wins the age. |
| Ta'veren | Deployments (and people) so architecturally significant that the Pattern bends around them. the Nansen is a ta'veren event. |
| The Wheel itself | Time + entropy: every closed architecture eventually fails from within; the mesh is the Wheel's natural expression because only distributed systems actually survive. |

The Forsaken are named in-universe as institutional roles only - never cartoonish villains. Each was, once, on the right side.

## The "One Power" split - disciplines as Saidin/Saidar

- **Protocol layer** (distributed systems architecture - gossip, lease, sync, partition recovery) = Saidin. Historically tainted by its association with infrastructure that can be weaponized. Requires naval-nuclear discipline to channel safely; otherwise corrupts the channeler.
- **Policy layer** (data sovereignty governance, key management, role architecture) = Saidar. Requires surrender before mastery: you have to understand what the data *means* to the person it belongs to before you can protect it - Sabina's Grameen framing is the clearest embodiment.

Joel channels Saidin. Wanjiru channels Saidar. Anna cannot channel either - she sees what the architecture does to real missions but cannot build it. That's her limitation and her power.

## Character → WoT role assignments

**Anna Yusupova = Moiraine Damodred**
The knower - recognized what Sunfish was before anyone else did; assembled the team; bet the mission on it. Second-attempt polar lead (post-Nimrod Shackleton) maps onto Moiraine after the Aiel Waste. She understands the stakes are civilizational, not operational. First-person narrator makes her more self-aware than Moiraine: the books are her reconstruction of events she survived.

**Joel Reyes = Rand al'Thor**
The Dragon Reborn analog - involuntary messianic status. Named the architecture after a submarine and published a paper; didn't sign up for what the paper means. The taint on Saidin: every prior architecture that deployed distributed systems without his lease + gossip protocols eventually became a vector for centralization. His R1 BLOCK night (sitting with Klett's verdict, knowing the hole, starting the rewrite before dawn) is the series' founding act of grace. Navy-nuclear discipline = the Three Oaths analog. Mid-50s; a Dragon who knows what he is and hates it.

**Priya Iyer = Nynaeve al'Meara**
Schema migration = Healing. The hardest discipline in the architecture. Her arc: from "the person who says no to schema changes" to "the person who makes the impossible migration work under the ice."

**Wanjiru Kamau = Egwene al'Vere**
Security architecture + key management = the political weave. She builds the institutions that outlast the mission. Most powerful Forsaken target in later books; she doesn't know this yet in Book 1. Eventually chairs the standards process equivalent.

**Sabina Rahman = Mat Cauthon**
Connectivity-gradient practitioner / Grameen use case. Her use-case authorship *shaped* the offline-first profile. Mat's "luck" maps to her specific kind of knowing: she doesn't design the protocol - she tells you whether it works in the real world. Her presence on the boat was Anna's eccentric choice; it turns out to be essential.

**Diego Vargas = Perrin Aybara**
Polar institutional memory. Pragmatic, physically capable, deeply competent. Heroism in the leak response = wolf-brother pragmatism. Retires to San Martín de los Andes - earned through what he did, not lost to it. The 4,017 photographs become life-affirming.

**Hiroshi Nakamura = Loial**
NIPR / Showa Station. Ancient institutional tradition. Knows where the prior architectures are buried. His arc spans timescales the others don't.

**Maria Santos = Min Farshaw**
She sees what others miss. Medical officer as moral register. Her "viewings" are diagnostic, not mystic - she reads bodies and behavior with clinical precision.

## The Bobiverse mechanic - replication and divergence

Every Sunfish deployment is a "Bob replication." The architecture clones itself into a new context; each instance faces different pressures; some diverge from founding principles. The series question: **what makes a deployment still Sunfish?**

Book 1 introduces this obliquely: the submarine partition (56-day radio blackout under ice) forces local operation. When the Nansen surfaces, it must reconcile 56 days of diverged state with the global mesh. The reconciliation scene is both technical and narrative - Anna's first external contact reveals the Forsaken moved while the boat was under the ice.

Later books: specific deployments go rogue through local optimization under extreme constraint. The "Bob who becomes Raimund" analog is a field deployment that ran in isolation so long it evolved incompatible governance primitives. Priya decides whether to merge it or fork it. This is the series' most technically and ethically loaded recurring conflict.

## Book 1 - *The Filchner Dark* (working title)

Anna's frame device: she's writing these logs *after the mission*, as part of required consortium documentation. She knows how it ends. Dramatic irony is structural.

**Act I (days 1-14):** Departure from Punta Arenas (06:42). Dive 11:18 day 1. Mesh signal lost ~0200 day 2; from here, Sunfish operates in partition. Crew dynamics establish. The architecture is theoretical - maiden voyage, nothing proven. Anna begins trusting Joel in real time; flashback fragments of the prior failure and the recruitment interview.

**Act II (days 15-42):** The work. Architecture-bearing chapters, each a dialogue between Anna and Joel as the system is tested by real data. One chapter per major subsystem (lease, gossip, schema migration, security/key management). Sabina's use-case validation. Hiroshi's Antarctic-scale data. Diego quietly essential in ways that become visible in Act III.

**Act III (days 43-56):** The leak. 0317 alarm → 0319 cabin-to-bridge → 0321 assessment → 0342 compartment isolation order. Fire cascade. Joel's nuclear-Navy training firing against his own architecture. Diego's competence saves the mission. Wanjiru's security layer holds when everything else degrades - her key management assumptions, written for political fragility in Kenya, happen to be exactly right for fire-and-flood on a submarine.

**Surfacing:** Day 56. Punta Arenas, 04:41. Reconciliation with mesh. First external contact reveals: while they were under the ice, the first formal regulatory challenge to local-first architecture was filed. PR #543 equivalent in-universe. The Forsaken are in the fight. Book 1 ends with the mission a success, the architecture proven, and the real contest beginning.

**The Spanish letter:** Anna writes to María Elena from Belgrano, in Spanish, explaining why Diego is delayed and what he did. Same kitchen table. Different message. The series' emotional anchor for what this is actually for.

## Series arc across books (WoT map)

| Book | WoT analog | Core move |
|---|---|---|
| 1: *The Filchner Dark* | Eye of the World | Architecture proved; Forsaken notice |
| 2 | The Great Hunt | First formal regulatory assault; a deployment compromised; who controls the proving artifact? |
| 3 | The Dragon Reborn | Joel's architecture reborn in second-generation form; Forsaken try to control the spec process |
| 4 | The Shadow Rising | Flashback/deep-lore: the Breaking fully revealed; Anna in the Aiel Waste equivalent (prior failure mission fully dramatized) |
| 5 | Fires of Heaven | Major network attack; mesh proves resilience; a beloved character is lost; Moiraine falls |
| 6 | Lord of Chaos | Regulatory offensive at full strength; "let the Lord of Chaos rule" = deliberately fragmenting the mesh |
| 7-8 | Path of Daggers / Winter's Heart | Wanjiru's arc; standards body; first legislative win |

Bobiverse threading: Bob-replication events happen in books 3-6, when deployments begin spinning off. By book 6, there are enough diverged instances that Priya's schema work is the series' structural spine. Nynaeve lifting the taint = Priya closing the final epoch incompatibility that would have forked the mesh permanently.

The centralized SaaS failure: if the Nansen is 2026, this was ~2022. The API went dark six days in. Anna doesn't name the vendor. Readers who were alive know exactly what she means.
