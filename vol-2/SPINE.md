## Back cover (locked)

Three hundred meters under the Antarctic ice, with a fire in the chemistry rack and twelve people breathing whatever's left in the boat, you find out fast who your friends are.

Mine, as it turned out, were complicated.

I'm **Dr. Anna Yusupova**, mission commander of the *Nansen*, and the official version of what happened next is in my staff history. The unofficial version starts at 03:17, with a sensor that had no business running at full duty for six hours, a vendor signature on a firmware push that cleared every consortium gate before departure, and the realization that **Priya Iyer** — my best chemist, my second on this boat — had killed rack power thirty seconds before I knew there was anything to kill.

She did everything right.

She did everything right *first.*

That, it turns out, is the tell.

**Joel Reyes** walked into the smoke while I held the passageway with a CO₂ extinguisher, which is a thing we will talk about eventually, if we both surface, which is not currently guaranteed. **Wanjiru Kamau's** survey lines do not point where she said they would. **Astrid Hansen** is running another boat under the same ice for a man who funded the rival expedition I didn't know existed — and Astrid was my apprentice once, which is the kind of detail you'd think I'd find more upsetting than I currently have time for.

Two betrayals. Two sponsors. Neither knew about the other.

I have a patched rack. A scrubbed atmosphere. Eleven people I am not going to bury at sea. The architecture Joel built is local, sovereign, and intact — and the logs are mine.

The *Nansen* surfaces in seventy-two hours.

I plan to surface with a list.

## Register commitments (load-bearing)

1. **First-person POV throughout.** Anna narrates. Past tense (she's writing this as staff history after the events).
2. **Conversational rhythm.** Sentences flow like speech. Parentheticals, em-dashes, "as it turns out," "which is to say."
3. **Wry, restrained humor.** Dry not pun-y. Anna comments on absurdities; she does not perform comedy.
4. **Technical content with confidence and accessibility.** Chemistry, sync architecture, mission ops — explained the way Bob explains von Neumann probes: like you're smart enough to follow, not like you need a primer.
5. **Stakes real, never solemn.** When a scene needs gravity, Anna's voice earns the shift by *dropping* the wry register, not by abandoning it.
6. **Romance as restrained subtext + occasional explicit beat.** Joel exists; the relationship exists; Anna will not narrate her feelings at length. She will narrate one specific thing she notices and let the reader do the rest.
7. **The niece (kindergarten, photograph of the *Nansen* above her bed) is the emotional anchor.** Recurs at chapter-spanning intervals, never as a long set piece.

## Plot anchors (locked)

- **Inciting incident:** 03:17 chemistry-rack fire — sabotage via firmware threshold alteration, signed through consortium vendor channel.
- **First betrayal:** Priya Iyer. Killed rack power thirty seconds before alarm. She knew. Different sponsor than Wanjiru.
- **Second betrayal:** Wanjiru Kamau. Survey-line adjustments steer *Nansen* toward bathymetry serving an undeclared interest.
- **External antagonist:** Astrid Hansen, running a parallel mission for Stefan Reinhardt. Anna's former apprentice. Goal: discredit Anna and bury the Sunfish architecture, not sink the boat.
- **Romance:** Joel Reyes. Prior encounter in Bremerhaven (consortium final review). Restrained on the page; emotionally load-bearing.
- **Crew:** twelve total. One will not survive. (Identity TBD — must be earned by character work, not assigned now.)
- **Window:** seventy-two hours to surface. Mission must complete; betrayals must be characterized; discreditation must be countered.
- **Climax shape:** race to surface, but the real race is for *credibility* — Anna's logs vs. Astrid's narrative.
- **Resolution shape:** Anna surfaces with the logs and a plan. Vol 1 ends with the first move in the credibility war. The war itself extends into Vol 2.

## Anti-patterns (what this book is not)

- Not airport thriller. Pace can move, but the voice does not punch.
- Not literary techno-thriller (Le Carré register). Voice is warmer, more accessible.
- Not procedural. Technical content serves voice and plot, not the other way around.
- Not romance-forward. The Joel arc is one of three structural beats, not the dominant register.
- Not grimdark. People die; Anna grieves; the book does not wallow.
