# Schema migration

**Plain replacement:** *record-shape rewrite* (most contexts) · *reformatting the records* (when the noun form reads stiff) · *the rewrite* (in tight prose where context is established)

**Longer gloss:** *Rewriting every stored record into a new shape - adding a field, renaming one, splitting a record in two - without losing any of them and without breaking the programs that read them.* (use at first occurrence in Ch 13; can be folded into Anna's interior at the refusal-of-record beat)

**Audio replacement:**
- *schema migration* → *record-shape rewrite* (audio always expands; the listener has zero anchor for *schema* and three wrong anchors for *migration* - birds, people moving, software relocation)
- *schema* alone → *record shape* or *data shape*
- *migration* alone (technical sense) → *the rewrite*
- *migration window* → *rewrite window*
- *schema-version handshake* → *record-shape version check*
- *schema-evolution* → *record-shape evolution* or *record-shape changes*
- *field-map* / *field map* → *the field-by-field map*

**Keep when:**
- Ch 13 chapter title (*The Schema That Should Not Migrate*) - canonical; the title's verb is load-bearing to the chapter's central question.
- Priya's, Joel's, or Anna's technical dialogue when one engineer is briefing another - engineer voice marker; they would say *schema migration*, not *record-shape rewrite*. Audio still expands at first occurrence.
- Anna's filed *refusal of record* and *yes-with-conditions* (Ch 13 opening log entries) - formal procedural artifacts inside the staff history; the technical term is part of the artifact's voice.
- Cross-references to Vol 1 Ch 13 *Schema Migration and Evolution* - keep the proper name in print; expand for audio.

**Mistaken for:**
- *Migration* (birds, people, software-to-new-server) - none of these. The non-technical reader's first association is wrong in three different directions; the longer gloss is essential at first occurrence.
- *Schema* (psychological / cognitive) - distinct meaning entirely. The technical sense is *the shape of a record: what fields it has, what type each field is*.
- *Database migration* (moving data to a new database engine) - distinct operation; don't conflate.

**Feynman test:** *Could a 10-year-old picture this?* Yes - "imagine every paper form in a giant filing cabinet has to be re-copied onto a new template - same information, new layout - without ever closing the office, without losing a single form, and without confusing the people who come in to read them." The recopy-the-filing-cabinet-while-the-office-is-open image is the workshop entry's load-bearing element.
