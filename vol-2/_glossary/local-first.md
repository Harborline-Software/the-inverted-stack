# Local-first

**Plain replacement:** *device-first* · *your-device-first* · *data-stays-with-you* · *local-first* (the term is plain enough on second occurrence in a chapter that has named it)

**Longer gloss:** *Local-first software is software where your data lives on your own device, not on a server somewhere else. Your computer is the primary place; the cloud is optional. You can keep working without internet because your computer doesn't need permission from a server to function. When the connection comes back, the system catches up.* (use at first occurrence in any chapter where the term is doing structural work - Vol 2 surfaces the term sparingly in body prose; encountered most often in surface-window press cycles, regulatory filings, and Stefan-rival framing)

**Audio replacement:**
- *local-first* → *local-first* on first occurrence with the longer gloss; thereafter unchanged. The hyphenated compound reads cleanly in audio.
- *local-first architecture* → *local-first architecture* - leave alone; the modifier *architecture* is what the listener uses to disambiguate from *local-first* in everyday senses (e.g., a *local-first* hiring policy at a community business).

**Keep when:**
- ALL occurrences. *Local-first* is the book's title concept. Replacement would erase the canonical name. The longer gloss is the workshop's primary contribution - it gives the listener a one-paragraph orientation without changing the term itself.
- Cross-references to Vol 1 (the entire book is the *local-first software* practitioner's guide; the term is canonical throughout).

**Mistaken for:**
- *Local-first hiring* / *local-first business* (community / labor-policy senses) - different domain entirely; the listener may need the longer gloss to reorient toward the software-architecture sense.
- *Offline-first* - closely related but distinct: *offline-first* is a design philosophy (the app should work offline first, online second); *local-first* is an architectural pattern (the data lives on the device first, the cloud second). Most readers conflate the two; for Vol 2 audiences this is acceptable conflation.
- *On-premises* / *self-hosted* - distinct concepts; *local-first* is about user devices (laptops, phones), not enterprise data centers. Don't conflate.

**Feynman test:** *Could a 10-year-old picture this?* Yes - "imagine the difference between writing in your own notebook versus writing on a chalkboard at school. Your notebook is in your backpack - you can take it home, write in it on the bus, write in it during a snowstorm when school is closed. The chalkboard at school only works when you're at school. Local-first software is the notebook version: your data is in your pocket, not at the school. The cloud is the chalkboard - useful when you want to share, but not where the writing lives." The notebook-versus-chalkboard image is the workshop entry's load-bearing element.
