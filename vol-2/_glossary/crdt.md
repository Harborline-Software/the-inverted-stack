# CRDT (Conflict-free Replicated Data Type)

**Plain replacement:** *the conflict-free data type* · *the merge-friendly data structure* · *the auto-merging records* · *CRDT* (only after expansion in same scene)

**Longer gloss:** *A CRDT — pronounced "C-R-D-T," short for Conflict-free Replicated Data Type — is a special kind of digital record that can be edited by multiple people on different devices at the same time, even when those devices can't talk to each other, and the system can always merge everyone's edits cleanly without losing anyone's work or asking a human to pick a winner. The architecture uses CRDTs for any data the crew might edit while the boat is offline.* (use at first occurrence in any chapter where the term is doing structural work — Vol 2 surfaces it through Joel and Priya's technical dialogue mostly)

**Audio replacement:**
- *CRDT* → *the conflict-free data type* on first occurrence in scene; *CRDT* afterward (the letter-by-letter pronunciation is fine on second occurrence; the listener has been primed)
- The bare letters *C-R-D-T* alone in audio (without the expanded name first) are vocabulary blanks — the listener has zero anchor.
- *CRDT engine* → *the conflict-free data engine*

**Keep when:**
- Engineer dialogue (Joel, Priya) — engineer voice marker; they would say *CRDT* in their working register. Audio expands at first occurrence.
- Cross-references to Vol 1 Ch 12 *CRDT Engine and Data Layer* — keep the proper noun in print; expand for audio.

**Mistaken for:**
- The bare letters *C-R-D-T* — non-technical reader has no association at all; listener has zero anchor.
- *CRT* (the old-style cathode-ray tube TV) — common audio mishearing of *CRDT*; clarify with the longer gloss.

**Feynman test:** *Could a 10-year-old picture this?* Yes — "imagine a notebook that can be in two friends' backpacks at the same time, and both friends can write in their copies whenever they want, even on a school bus with no signal. When they meet up at the playground, the notebook auto-merges everything they each wrote — without asking, without losing anything, without making them argue about whose version wins. A CRDT is the trick that makes that possible." The two-notebooks-that-auto-merge image is the workshop entry's load-bearing element.
