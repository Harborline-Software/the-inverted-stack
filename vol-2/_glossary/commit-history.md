# Commit history

**Plain replacement:** *the change history* · *the version history* · *the chronological record of edits*

**Longer gloss:** *In the version-control software engineers use to manage code, every saved change is called a "commit," and each commit captures who made the change, when, and what they changed. The complete chronological record of all commits to a piece of software is called the commit history. It's an audit trail for the code itself.* (use at first occurrence in Ch 1 - Joel discloses *the full commit history of his Sunfish work going back five years, including the Round Two rewrite*)

**Audio replacement:**
- *commit history* → *change history* on first occurrence in scene; *commit history* afterwards
- *the commit* (technical noun) → *the saved change* or *the recorded change*
- *committed forward* / *roll forward* → already covered by `rollback.md` substitutions

**Keep when:**
- Joel's Ch 1 disclosure to Anna - the engineer's professional register would say *commit history*.
- Anna's filed log entries.

**Mistaken for:**
- *Commit* (the everyday verb - to commit to a decision, to commit a crime) - distinct senses; the technical sense is narrower.
- *History* (academic history, family history) - the technical sense is *the change-by-change record of one piece of software over time*.

**Feynman test:** *Could a 10-year-old picture this?* Yes - "imagine a story you write in a notebook, and every time you change a sentence, you write down what you changed and why on a separate page. Years later, you can flip through those pages and see exactly when each sentence got its current shape. The commit history is the same idea for a piece of software." The story-edit-diary image is the workshop entry's load-bearing element.
