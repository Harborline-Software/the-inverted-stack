# Bridge (Sunfish accelerator)

**Plain replacement:** *the Sunfish Bridge platform* (first occurrence in any scene) · *the Bridge service* · *the Bridge* (in tight prose where the Sunfish meaning is already established and the boat's command bridge is not currently on the page)

**Longer gloss:** *Sunfish Bridge is the part of the architecture that connects your device to the cloud — when changes are ready to leave your machine and travel to a shared service, they cross the Bridge; when shared changes need to come back to your machine, they cross it the other way.* (use at first occurrence in any chapter where the architectural Bridge is doing structural work — Ch 1 Wanjiru handoff [Bridge replication credentials], Ch 6 first surface [sync-replication-to-Bridge], Ch 18 docking [the Bridge restores full capability])

**Audio replacement:**
- *Bridge* (Sunfish meaning) → ALWAYS expand to *the Sunfish Bridge platform* or *the Bridge service* in audio. The listener has no capital letter to disambiguate from the boat's command bridge; bare *Bridge* in audio is indistinguishable from *bridge*.
- *bridge* (boat command bridge) → leave alone. Context (*Anna stood on the bridge*, *I needed to be on the bridge*) makes the meaning unambiguous in audio.
- *sync-replication-to-Bridge* → *catch-up replication to the Sunfish Bridge platform*
- *Bridge mirror* → *the Sunfish Bridge mirror copy*
- *Bridge replication credentials* → *the Sunfish Bridge platform credentials*
- *the Bridge restores* / *the Bridge fills* (Ch 18) → *the Sunfish Bridge platform restores* / *the Sunfish Bridge platform fills*

**Keep when:**
- Sentences where the boat's command bridge is the meaning — these stay as lowercase *bridge* in print and unmodified in audio. Example (Ch 6): *a Mission Director who needed to be on the bridge and not in the staff history.* Audio listener parses correctly from *be on the* + *bridge*.
- Cross-references to Vol 1 where *Bridge* is the canonical Sunfish accelerator name — print keeps the proper noun; audio still expands at first occurrence in each chapter.
- Engineer dialogue (Wanjiru, Joel, Priya) when one engineer names the architectural Bridge to another — engineer voice marker. Audio still expands.

**Mistaken for:**
- The boat's command bridge — the dominant collision in Vol 2. Anna stands on the boat's bridge constantly; the Sunfish Bridge is a different thing in the same chapter. Audio readers especially will conflate the two without explicit modifier.
- *Bridge* as in *card-game bridge* / *suspension bridge* / *Brooklyn Bridge* — a child or non-technical reader who has never encountered the Sunfish meaning will reach for everyday associations first. The longer gloss handles this at first occurrence.
- *Bridging two networks* (the technical sense of *bridge* in networking, distinct from Sunfish Bridge as a product) — close enough that engineers sometimes conflate; but Sunfish Bridge is a hybrid local-cloud coordinator, not a network bridge. Don't substitute *network bridge*.

**Feynman test:** *Could a 10-year-old picture this?* Yes — "imagine a real bridge between two banks of a river: your information walks across the bridge from your computer to the cloud and back. The Bridge is the part of the system that makes that crossing possible." The bridge-between-two-banks image is the workshop entry's load-bearing element — and it's the *same* picture as the Sunfish product name's metaphor, which is why renaming the product would cost more than it would save.
