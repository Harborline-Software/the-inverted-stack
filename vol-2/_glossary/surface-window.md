# Surface window (surface comms)

**Plain replacement:** *the surfacing window* · *the communication window* · *the surface communication window* (when the operational-procedural noun is needed) · *the surface window* (the term is plain enough once defined)

**Longer gloss:** *The boat operates submerged for weeks at a time and cannot send or receive anything from the outside world while submerged. Three times during the mission it surfaces for forty-seven hours so the crew can send their accumulated findings up to shore and receive whatever shore has tried to send down. Those forty-seven-hour periods are called surface windows. They are short, they are scheduled, and the queue of what gets sent is triaged by priority because the bandwidth is finite.* (use at first occurrence in any chapter where the term is doing structural work — Ch 6 first surface, Ch 11 second surface, Ch 17 transit north)

**Audio replacement:**
- *surface window* → *surfacing window* in most contexts (slight rephrasing prevents the listener from picturing a literal window on the boat's surface)
- *the first surface window* / *the second surface window* / *the third surface window* → leave alone after first occurrence in chapter; the ordinal makes the operational sense clear
- *surface comms* → *surface communications*
- *during the surface* → *during the surfacing window*
- *at surface* (when used as a procedural noun) → *at the surfacing window* or *during the surfacing window*

**Keep when:**
- Engineer dialogue and Anna's filed log entries — operational-procedural register.
- Cross-references between Ch 6 / Ch 11 / Ch 17 where the *first / second / third surface window* triplet structures the mission's communication arc.
- Chapter titles where *Surface* is the structuring word (Ch 6 *First Surface*, Ch 11 *Second Surface*, Ch 18 *Punta Arenas Surfacing*).

**Mistaken for:**
- *Window* (the architectural / vehicle / computer-display senses) — the listener may picture a glass pane; the term is operational-procedural, not physical.
- *Window of opportunity* (idiomatic) — closer to the right meaning; partial transfer.
- *Surface* as a verb (the boat surfaces) — the noun phrase *surface window* is operational-procedural; the verb *surface* is physical. Both senses appear; context disambiguates.

**Feynman test:** *Could a 10-year-old picture this?* Yes — "imagine a submarine that can only call home once a month, and only for two days at a time. Those two days are the surface window. Everything the submarine wants to tell home, and everything home wants to tell back, has to fit inside those two days. So the crew makes a list ahead of time of what's most important and sends those things first." The submarine-can-only-call-home-twice-a-month image is the workshop entry's load-bearing element.
