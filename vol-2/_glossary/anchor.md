# Anchor (Sunfish accelerator)

**Plain replacement:** *the Sunfish Anchor service* (first occurrence in any scene) · *the Anchor system* · *the Anchor* (in tight prose where the Sunfish meaning is already established)

**Longer gloss:** *Sunfish Anchor is the part of the architecture that lives on your own device — it holds your data right there, where you are, so you can keep working even when there's no internet. Nothing has to leave your machine for the system to function.* (use at first occurrence in any chapter where the architectural Anchor is doing structural work — Ch 1 Wanjiru handoff [Anchor model weights' integrity-keys], Ch 6 first surface [the sweep is in the Anchor archive], Ch 14 leak event, Ch 16 final ascent)

**Audio replacement:**
- *Anchor* (Sunfish meaning) → ALWAYS expand to *the Sunfish Anchor service* or *the Anchor system* in audio. The listener has no capital letter to disambiguate from the boat's anchor; bare *Anchor* in audio is indistinguishable from *anchor*.
- *anchor* (boat anchor) → leave alone. Submarines do not anchor underwater, but the Nansen anchors at the consortium pier (Ch 1 departure, Ch 18 docking); the context makes the everyday meaning clear.
- *Anchor model weights* → *the Sunfish Anchor service's model weights* or *the on-device model weights*
- *the Anchor archive* → *the Sunfish Anchor archive* or *the on-device archive*
- *integrity-keys for the Anchor model* → *the digital keys for the Sunfish Anchor model*

**Keep when:**
- Sentences where the boat's anchor is the meaning — these stay as lowercase *anchor* in print and unmodified in audio. *The pier crew dropped the anchor* / *the boat had not yet weighed anchor* parse correctly without modification.
- Cross-references to Vol 1 where *Anchor* is the canonical Sunfish accelerator name — print keeps the proper noun; audio still expands at first occurrence in each chapter.
- Engineer dialogue (Wanjiru, Joel, Priya) when one engineer names the architectural Anchor to another — engineer voice marker. Audio still expands.

**Mistaken for:**
- The boat's anchor — direct collision; less frequent in Vol 2 than the Bridge collision (the Nansen is submerged for most of the mission and does not anchor while submerged) but still present at the pier scenes (Ch 1 departure, Ch 18 docking).
- *News anchor* / *anchor of the team* (figurative) — non-technical reader's secondary association. The longer gloss handles this at first occurrence.
- *Anchor text* / *anchor link* (web-development sense) — distinct from Sunfish Anchor; engineers should not conflate.

**Feynman test:** *Could a 10-year-old picture this?* Yes — "imagine a heavy anchor that holds a boat in one place so it doesn't drift away with the current. The Anchor holds your data in one place — on your own device — so it doesn't drift away to the cloud unless you decide to send it." The anchor-holds-the-boat image is the workshop entry's load-bearing element — and like Bridge, it's the *same* picture the Sunfish product name's metaphor uses, which is why renaming would cost more than it would save.
