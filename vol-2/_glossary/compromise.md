# Compromise (compromised, supply-chain compromise)

**Plain replacement:** *attacked* · *broken into* · *infiltrated* · *secretly altered* (when the silent-alteration sense is meant)

**Longer gloss:** *In the security sense, a system is compromised when an attacker has gained access to it without authorization - when the attacker can read what they shouldn't, change what they shouldn't, or impersonate someone they're not. A compromised system may keep operating normally on the surface while doing something it wasn't supposed to do underneath.* (use at first occurrence in any chapter where the security sense is doing structural work - Ch 14 leak event, Ch 15 *Compromised Relay Holds*, Ch 17 transit north [Wanjiru's forensic supply-chain recognition])

**Audio replacement:**
- *compromised* (security sense, adjective) → *attacked* or *broken into* on first occurrence in scene; *compromised* afterwards
- *the compromised relay* → already covered by `relay.md` substitution (*compromised message-relay server*)
- *supply-chain compromise* → *supply-chain attack* (the listener's anchor for *compromise* is the political-deal sense; *attack* is unambiguous)
- *supply-chain* (used adjectivally) → leave alone if context already established
- *compromise* used in the political/agreement sense (a *compromise* between two parties) → leave alone; the meaning is clear from context

**Keep when:**
- Wanjiru's dialogue and interior in Ch 15 / Ch 17 - engineer voice marker; she would say *compromised* in her professional register. Audio still expands at first occurrence per the rules above.
- Ch 15 chapter title (*Compromised Relay Holds*) - title is canonical; chapter-internal references can be replaced after the title sets the term. The audio narrator paces *Compromised Relay Holds* so the listener registers *Compromised* as adjective, *Relay* as noun, *Holds* as verb.
- Cross-references to Vol 1 Ch 7 *The Security Lens* and Ch 22 *Key Lifecycle Operations*.

**Mistaken for:**
- *Compromise* (a political deal - *they reached a compromise*) - the dominant collision; the everyday meaning is the OPPOSITE valence (peaceful agreement vs hostile breach). The longer gloss is essential at first occurrence.
- *Compromise* (a moral compromise - *I won't compromise on this*) - distinct register; the listener will reach for this when the technical sense is intended.
- *Compromise* (a degraded but functional state - *we had to compromise on the design*) - closer to the security sense in valence (something less than ideal) but still distinct.

**Feynman test:** *Could a 10-year-old picture this?* Yes - "imagine someone has secretly made a copy of your house key and now they can come into your house anytime they want, even though you didn't give them permission. Your house isn't broken - the front door still locks, the windows are still shut - but the security has been compromised, because someone who shouldn't have access does." The secret-copy-of-the-house-key image is the workshop entry's load-bearing element.
