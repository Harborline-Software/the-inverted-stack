# Telemetry

**Plain replacement:** *the system's self-reporting* · *the operational readings* · *the system's diagnostic data* · *telemetry* (the term has partial reader-anchor from spaceflight)

**Longer gloss:** *Telemetry is the steady stream of measurements a system reports about itself while it's running — how busy it is, how much memory it's using, how many requests it's handling, how long each request took, what errors it has seen. The crew uses telemetry to know whether the architecture is healthy without having to interrupt it to ask.* (use at first occurrence in any chapter where the term is doing structural work — Vol 2 surfaces *telemetry* in operational-triage scenes)

**Audio replacement:**
- *telemetry* → *the system's self-reporting* on first occurrence in scene; *telemetry* afterwards
- The bare word *telemetry* has partial transfer from spaceflight context (NASA Mission Control's *we're losing telemetry from the spacecraft*); a Vol 2 listener may anchor here, which is roughly correct.

**Keep when:**
- Engineer dialogue (Wanjiru, Joel, Priya).
- Anna's filed log entries.

**Mistaken for:**
- *Telepathy* — audio mishearing; flag if the narrator's pacing seems to allow the confusion.
- *Telephoto* / *teleconference* — partial-prefix mishearings; usually corrected by context.

**Feynman test:** *Could a 10-year-old picture this?* Yes — "imagine a fitness watch that reports your heart rate, steps, and calories every minute, sending the readings to your phone where you can see them on a chart. Telemetry is the same idea for a computer system — it reports its own vital signs while it works." The fitness-watch image is the workshop entry's load-bearing element.
