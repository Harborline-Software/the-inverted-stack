# Endpoint

**Plain replacement:** *the address* · *the destination* · *the connection point* · *the endpoint* (only in tight technical-procedural contexts where the noun is canonical)

**Longer gloss:** *An endpoint is the specific address a system uses to send messages or requests to another system — the digital equivalent of a phone number plus a person's name at the receiving end. Different endpoints on the same system handle different kinds of requests.* (use at first occurrence in any chapter where the term is doing structural work — appears most often in Vol 2 in security / custody contexts cross-referencing Vol 1 Ch 23)

**Audio replacement:**
- *endpoint* → *the address* or *the connection point* (audio always; the listener has no everyday anchor for *endpoint* in its technical sense)
- *the relay endpoint* → *the relay-server address*
- *the consortium's endpoint* → *the consortium's address*
- *endpoint compromise* (Vol 1 cross-reference; security sense) → *device compromise* or *endpoint device compromise*

**Keep when:**
- Cross-references to Vol 1 Ch 23 *Endpoint, Collaborator, and Custody Operations* — keep the proper-noun-style title in print; expand for audio.
- Engineer dialogue when *endpoint* is the precise technical noun.

**Mistaken for:**
- *End point* (the end of something, the terminal point) — partial transfer; the technical sense is *a connection point*, which is more specific.
- *Endpoint* (medical / pharmaceutical — a primary outcome measured in a clinical trial) — wrong domain; flag if the reader/listener seems to drift.

**Feynman test:** *Could a 10-year-old picture this?* Yes — "imagine your friend's house has three doors: front door for friends, side door for the dog, back door for the gardener. Each door is an endpoint — a specific way in for a specific purpose. The address of each door is what makes them findable." The friend's-house-with-three-doors image is the workshop entry's load-bearing element.
